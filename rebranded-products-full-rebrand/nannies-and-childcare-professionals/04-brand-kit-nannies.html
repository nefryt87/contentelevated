Brand Kit + Professional Profile Guide

Two signature palettes, warm-professional typography, 7 Canva and platform template strategies, and a 7-day profile launch checklist

WHY YOUR PROFESSIONAL PRESENCE GETS

YOU SHORTLISTED

Families shortlist nannies based on their profile, their photo, and the first impression of their communication before they ever have a conversation. A polished, warm, and consistent professional presence — on platforms, on LinkedIn, and in your written

materials — communicates the same qualities that make a great nanny: care, organisation, warmth, and professionalism. This kit gives you two palettes, typography pairings, and template strategies built specifically for nannies and childcare professionals.

your key certifications. Every parent or agency who visits your LinkedIn sees this first — make it professional, warm, and immediately credible.

Template 2: Nanny CV / Profile Document

Search 'nanny CV template' or 'childcare professional resume'. Clean, warm layout. Your photo (professional, warm). Your name and contact. Your summary (use your platform bio). Experience (reverse chronological, with ages of children cared for).

Qualifications and certifications. References available on request. Keep to 1-2 pages — concise and readable. Update it every 6 months.

Template 3: Credential / Certification Graphic

Search 'certification badge Instagram' or 'professional credentials post'. A clean graphic featuring your key credentials: 'First Aid Certified', 'DBS Cleared', 'Paediatric CPR', '[X] Years Experience'. Your name. Use as a pinned Instagram post, on your LinkedIn, and

in your platform profile. Parents scan this type of content quickly — make it visually easy to read.

Template 4: Activity Idea Post

Search 'children activity Instagram' or 'parenting tip post'. A clean, engaging graphic: '[Age] Activity: [Activity Name]' with a brief description. Your name or handle. Post these regularly — parents save them, share them, and remember the professional who

consistently provides value. Positions you as an expert, not just a carer.

Template 5: Testimonial / Reference Graphic

Search 'professional testimonial Instagram' or 'nanny review post'. Feature a specific, warm quote from a past family (with their permission). Their first name and children's ages (optional). Your name. These are your most powerful trust signals — post every

new reference or testimonial you receive. Parents trust other parents more than any other marketing.

Template 6: Availability Announcement

Search 'professional available post' or 'nanny available LinkedIn'. Clean graphic: 'NOW AVAILABLE: [Role Type] in [Area] from [Date]'. Your key credentials (experience level, certifications, DBS). Your contact details. Post this on LinkedIn, Instagram, and update

your platform profile simultaneously every time you open for new inquiries.

Template 7: Instagram Story — Daily Dispatch

Search 'daily update story template' or 'professional story'. Vertical. Your name or business name small at top. 'Today's adventures:' with a safe, consent-appropriate image or graphic (no identifiable children unless fully consented). A warm caption. Post

daily when working — parents of prospective clients follow nannies to see their day-to-

day, and this builds enormous trust over time.

7-DAY PROFESSIONAL PROFILE LAUNCH

CHECKLIST

✓ Day 1: Choose your palette. Arrange a professional headshot if your current photo

is not warm and current.

✓ Day 2: Rewrite your Care.com / UrbanSitter / Sittercity profile using the bio from

Chapter 3 of Doc 1.

✓ Day 3: Update your LinkedIn — new headline, new summary (Chapter 5 prompt

from Doc 1), and your new banner (Template 1).

✓ Day 4: Create your credential graphic using Template 3 — add to Instagram,

LinkedIn, and your platform profile.

✓ Day 5: Create 5 activity idea posts using Template 4 — schedule one per week for 5

weeks.

✓ Day 6: Request written references from 2-3 past families using the Chapter 3

template from Doc 3.

✓ Day 7: Post your availability announcement using Template 6 — LinkedIn,

Instagram, and update all platform statuses.

✓ [A professional, consistent online presence does not guarantee the right family —

but it gets you in front of them. Families are making a high-trust decision and your professional presentation is the first signal they use to decide who to shortlist.]
