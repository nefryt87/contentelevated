BRAND KIT & CANVA TEMPLATE GUIDE

Build a nutrition brand that attracts ideal clients and commands premium rates. Color palettes • Fonts • Canva templates • Niche clarity framework • 7-day checklist

SECTION 1: BRAND POSITIONING

The nutrition space is crowded with generic advice. The practitioners who build thriving practices are those who are clearly positioned — who they help, what outcome they deliver, and why their approach is different. Your brand communicates this before a

potential client ever speaks to you. Your Practice Promise I help [specific person — e.g., women with PCOS / endurance athletes / people newly diagnosed with type 2 diabetes / busy professionals] [achieve specific

outcome — e.g., balance their hormones through food / fuel their training and recover faster / manage their blood sugar without medication / finally have a healthy relationship with food] without [biggest fear — e.g., extreme restriction /

giving up the foods they love / complicated meal plans they can't sustain]. Example: 'I help women with PCOS balance their hormones and lose weight sustainably — without cutting out entire food groups or following a plan that doesn't fit real life.'

Niche Clarity — The Most Important Brand Decision You'll Make Generalist nutritionists compete on price. Specialists in high-demand niches command 2–3x higher rates and attract clients who are pre-sold on their expertise. You don't have

to turn anyone away — but your marketing should speak to one person.

• Gut health and IBS / IBD

• Diabetes and metabolic health

• PCOS and hormonal balance

• Sports nutrition and performance

• Weight management (non-diet approach)

• Prenatal and postnatal nutrition

• Eating disorder recovery support

• Pediatric and family nutrition

• Plant-based and vegan nutrition

• Oncology nutrition support

SECTION 3: TYPOGRAPHY

USE FONT NOTES

Headlines (Clinical) Josefin Sans Bold Clean, modern authority — feels scientific without being cold. Free on Google Fonts. Headlines (Holistic) Playfair Display Bold Elegant and warm — ideal for wellness, non- diet, or hormone-focused brands.

Body Copy Karla Regular Highly readable and friendly — excellent for

client-facing content.

CTA / Emphasis Josefin Sans SemiBold For buttons, key stats, and calls to action. Use sparingly.

SECTION 5: CONTENT PHOTO & VIDEO GUIDE

What to Photograph

• Food styling: Real food, natural light, simple props — avoid overly staged or

filtered images

• Your workspace: Clean desk, notebook, laptop — signals professionalism and

approachability

• Headshot: Warm, genuine, ideally in a natural or clean clinical setting — not a

white coat unless appropriate for your brand

• Process shots: Reviewing a meal plan, having a virtual session, cooking demo —

shows clients what working with you looks like Short-Form Video Ideas (Instagram Reels / TikTok)

• 'Reacting to viral nutrition advice' — one of the highest-reach formats for

dietitians

• 'What I eat in a day as a dietitian' — high curiosity, strong trust-builder

• 'Grocery store walkthrough' — incredibly practical, very shareable

• 'Explaining [condition] nutrition in 60 seconds' — establishes niche authority fast

• 'Client win story' (with consent) — emotional, high-conversion content
