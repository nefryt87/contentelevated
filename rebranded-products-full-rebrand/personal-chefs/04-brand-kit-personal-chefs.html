PERSONAL CHEF

Brand Kit + Canva Template Guide

Two signature palettes, culinary typography, 7 Canva template strategies, and a 7-day brand launch checklist

WHY YOUR BRAND COMMUNICATES YOUR

CALIBRE BEFORE YOUR FOOD DOES

High-value clients hiring a private chef make their first judgement based on your digital presence — your website, your Instagram, your email signature. A polished, cohesive visual identity communicates the same thing as a beautifully plated dish: precision, care,

and a standard worth paying for. This kit gives you two palettes, typography pairings, and Canva strategies built specifically for personal chefs and private dining professionals.

Typography

Typography

HEADINGS: Cormorant Garamond — editorial luxury serif. The typography of high-end hospitality. BODY: Source Sans Pro — professional and clean. Reads beautifully at all sizes. ACCENT: Cormorant Garamond Italic — for seasonal menu titles, evocative dish descriptions, testimonials.

7 CANVA TEMPLATE STRATEGIES

Template 1: Food Photography Post with Minimal Overlay

Search 'food photography Instagram post' or 'restaurant food post template'. Your best dish photography fills the frame. Minimal text overlay: your name or studio in small type, bottom corner. Clean and editorial. Food photography is your portfolio — let the image

do the work. Create 10 of these with consistent overlay style.

Template 2: Seasonal Menu Card

Search 'restaurant menu design' or 'dinner menu template'. A beautifully formatted seasonal menu — courses listed elegantly. Your name at top, seasonal notation at bottom. Use Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond for the dish names. Post as an

Instagram feed image and story slide when launching a seasonal menu. Pin to Pinterest.

Template 3: Behind-the-Scenes Story Template

Search 'food story template' or 'chef behind the scenes story'. Vertical format. Your photo at a farmers market, in the kitchen, or plating. Brief overlay text: 'Preparing for tonight's private dinner' or 'This morning's market haul.' Your name. Post to stories

several times per week — it humanises the experience.

Template 4: Client Testimonial Graphic

Search 'food testimonial quote' or 'client review restaurant post'. Feature the most sensory and specific line from a client's review. Their name (or 'Private Dining Client, [City]'). Your business name. These build trust faster than any other content type —

schedule one per week.

Template 5: Pinterest Seasonal Menu Board

Search 'dinner party menu board' or 'entertaining ideas Pinterest'. Create boards for each season and occasion: 'Spring Dinner Party Menus', 'Intimate Winter Dining', 'Al Fresco Summer Entertaining'. Pin your menu cards, food photography, and ingredient

content. Pinterest drives long-tail discovery for event planners and high-value clients.

Template 6: Instagram Story — Availability CTA

Search 'booking story template' or 'chef availability story'. Vertical. A beautiful atmospheric dining or food photo as background. Text: 'Taking bookings for [month/season]' + 'Private dinners, events, weekly meal prep.' + 'Enquire via link.' Your

Template 7: Gift Experience Card

7-DAY BRAND LAUNCH CHECKLIST

✓ Day 1: Choose your palette and set up Canva brand kit (colours + fonts saved).

✓ Day 2: Update Instagram bio, profile photo, and create 6 story highlight covers:

Portfolio, Events, Meal Prep, Reviews, Seasonal Menus, Booking.

✓ Day 3: Create 10 food photography post overlays using Template 1 — apply to your

10 best food photos.

✓ Day 4: Create a seasonal menu card using Template 2 — your current or upcoming

seasonal menu.

✓ Day 5: Create 5 testimonial graphics using Template 4 — schedule one per week

for 5 weeks.

✓ Day 6: Create a gift experience card using Template 7 — share before the next

occasion or holiday.

✓ Day 7: Create a Pinterest account if you don't have one. Set up 4 seasonal boards

and pin 10 images to each.

✓ [A professional brand across Instagram, Pinterest, and email communicates the

premium standard of your food before a client has ever tasted it.]
