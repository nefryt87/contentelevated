SECTION 1: HOME EXERCISE PROGRAM

TRACKING

HEP Weekly Adherence Check-In

SUBJECT Quick HEP check-in — [First Name]

Hi [First Name], Just a quick mid-week check on your home exercises: 1. Have you been able to complete your HEP this week? (All / Most / Some / None) 2. Any exercises that feel unclear, uncomfortable, or that you have questions about?

3. Any changes in your symptoms since your last visit (better / same / worse)? Even a quick one-word reply helps me prepare for your next session. Thank you! [Your Name], PT | [Practice Name] HEP Non-Adherence Compassionate Reframe

SUBJECT Quick note on your home exercises — [First Name]

Hi [First Name], I know home exercises can be hard to stick with — life gets in the way and I understand that completely. I do want to be honest with you: the research on your condition is really clear that HEP adherence is one of the strongest predictors of recovery. The work

we do in the clinic is valuable — but the daily work at home is what locks in the progress. If there's something making it hard — time, pain, not remembering, not understanding a specific exercise — let's fix that at your next session. I'd

rather adjust the program to something you'll actually do than keep prescribing something you won't. See you [date]. [Your Name], PT | [Practice Name]
