ACCOUNTANT & CPA

The AI Playbook for Accountants & CPAs Convert more consultations, retain clients through life events, build referral partnerships with attorneys and financial advisors, and become the most trusted advisor in your clients' lives

INTRODUCTION

The CPA practices growing fastest aren't just technically superior — they communicate better. They follow up on consultations before the prospect has time to contact anyone else. They reach out proactively when a tax law changes that affects their clients. They

maintain attorney and financial advisor relationships with a monthly value touch that generates referrals year-round. And they show up digitally in ways that make their ideal clients find them before they ever search a competitor. This playbook gives you 7

chapters covering every high-leverage communication scenario — with copy-paste AI prompts throughout.

CHAPTER 1: CONSULTATION CONVERSION &

PROSPECT NURTURING

The Post-Consultation Follow-Up System The consultation that doesn't convert in 48 hours rarely converts at all. Most prospects are contacting 2-3 CPAs. The first professional, specific, and action-oriented follow-up wins a disproportionate number of engagements.

PROMPT — POST-CONSULTATION FOLLOW-UP EMAIL

Write a post-consultation follow-up email for my CPA practice. The prospect [Name] came in about [matter type — e.g., business tax planning / personal tax return / estate planning / new business setup]. Key things they shared: [list 2-3 things from

the consultation]. I want to: (1) summarize their situation in plain language showing I listened, (2) outline my recommended approach in 2-3 sentences, (3) present a clear next step and fee expectation. Professional but warm. Under 200 words. Sign

as [Your Name], [Firm Name]. Note: review for compliance with your state bar and professional standards.

The Referral Source Introduction System One strong attorney or financial advisor relationship maintained well can send 5-15 qualified referrals per year. The difference between one who refers once and one who refers consistently is regular, professional contact.

PROMPT — ATTORNEY / FINANCIAL ADVISOR INTRODUCTION

Write a professional introduction email from a CPA to [an attorney / a financial advisor] named [Name] at [Firm]. I specialise in [specialty — e.g., small business tax / estate planning / high-net-worth individuals]. I want to: (1) introduce myself and my

practice briefly, (2) explain how our clients likely overlap and how we can help each other's clients, (3) propose a brief coffee or call. Under 150 words. Professional. Sign as [Your Name], [Firm Name].

PROMPT — MONTHLY PROFESSIONAL REFERRAL VALUE TOUCH

Write a monthly value email from a CPA to a referral partner — [attorney / financial advisor] named [Name] at [Firm]. Include: (1) one brief tax or accounting insight relevant to their clients right now — e.g., a recent IRS update, a planning

opportunity, or a common issue I'm seeing, (2) a soft mention of any relevant case type I'm available for. Professional, genuinely useful, under 150 words. Should feel like useful information from a knowledgeable colleague, not a marketing email. Sign as [Your Name], [Firm Name].

LIFE EVENTS

The Life Event Outreach System CPAs who build lifelong client relationships show up at the right moments — without being asked. Five life events trigger the most valuable tax and planning conversations:

marriage, divorce, new child, inheritance, and business sale. Most CPAs wait for clients to call them. The best ones reach out first.

Tax season is your highest-touch client period — but most of the stress comes from

poor communication about what's needed and when. A proactive communication

system sets expectations, reduces back-and-forth, and positions your firm as organised

and professional.

PROMPT — TAX SEASON KICKOFF EMAIL

PROMPT — MISSING DOCUMENTS FOLLOW-UP

miss the deadline — extension, etc. Professional and direct without being harsh. Under 120 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Firm Name].

Moving Clients From Compliance to Advisory The most profitable CPA engagements are advisory — tax planning, business consulting, wealth strategy — not just compliance. The challenge is that most clients

don't know to ask for it, and most CPAs don't know how to introduce it without it feeling like a sales pitch.

PROMPT — ADVISORY SERVICES INTRODUCTION EMAIL

Write an email introducing advisory/tax planning services to an existing compliance

client [Name] who is a [business owner / high-income individual / recent inheritance

recipient]. I want to frame advisory services as: 'this is what we do for clients who want to get ahead of their tax situation rather than just report it.' Include 1-2 specific examples of planning opportunities relevant to their situation. Invite a 30-minute

planning call. Not salesy — framed as genuinely serving their interests. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Firm Name].

PROMPT — FEE INCREASE COMMUNICATION

Write a professional fee increase notification for my CPA practice to send to existing clients. Our fees are increasing by [X]% effective [date] due to [brief reason — increased complexity of regulations, cost of technology, investment in staff training].

I want to: (1) communicate the change clearly, (2) reaffirm the value of the relationship, (3) invite questions. Professional, warm, and confident. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Firm Name].

PROMPT — MONTHLY CLIENT NEWSLETTER

ask for a Google review, (3) include the direct link. Warm and brief. Under 120 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Firm Name]. Responding to Reviews
