WHY YOUR BRAND MATTERS MORE THAN YOUR PRICE

Clients don't just buy insurance — they buy confidence. When your LinkedIn profile looks inconsistent, your email signature is missing, and your proposals are plain docs, you're signaling 'small-time operation.' A

consistent brand signals credibility, trustworthiness, and professionalism — the exact qualities clients need before trusting you with their employees' health coverage decisions. THE BRAND TRUST RULE: It takes 5–7 brand impressions before someone remembers you. Every

touchpoint — your email, social post, proposal, voicemail — is a chance to reinforce that you're the expert they should trust.

YOUR BRAND VOICE: FINDING YOUR TONE

As a solo broker, YOU are your brand. Your voice should feel like a knowledgeable friend — someone who simplifies the complicated and always has their client's best interest at heart.

SOUND LIKE THIS ✓

NOT LIKE THIS ✗

'Here's what this means for YOUR business...' 'Per our policy documentation and carrier guidelines...' 'This is a great option if you have a younger workforce' 'This product is competitively positioned in the current market' 'Let me simplify this for you'

'The actuarial components of this plan...' 'I've seen this save clients $20K — here's how' 'Clients have reported various cost savings outcomes' 'Quick question for you...' (invites engagement) 'Please contact our office to schedule a consultation'

HIPAA-SAFE CONTENT: DOS AND DON'TS

✓ DO

✗ DON'T

Share general education about plan types, costs, and enrollment timelines Share any client-specific health or claims information — ever Use anonymized case studies ('A client in manufacturing saved 22%...') Name clients or industries that could identify them

without written consent Post statistics from public sources (KFF, CMS, SHRM) Screenshot or share carrier documents marked 'confidential' Share client testimonials with written permission Share photos of client team events with employees without consent
