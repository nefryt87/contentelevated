AI Prompt Cheat Sheet 10 AI prompts to fill your practice, retain clients longer, and launch your next program

How to use these prompts

Copy and paste into Claude or ChatGPT. Replace [brackets] with your details. Each prompt takes under 5 minutes to run — and replaces 30-60 minutes of writing.

Client Acquisition Prompts

Discovery Call Follow-Up Write a post-discovery-call conversion email for a [life / business / executive] coach. The prospect [Name] shared these goals on the call: [list 2-3 goals]. Mirror their exact language. Propose [package name] at $[amount]. Make the next step

(signing up) feel natural and low-pressure. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name]. 30-Day Nurture Email Write a 30-day follow-up email for a coaching prospect [Name] who said they weren't ready yet after a discovery call. Share one relevant insight or resource. Keep the

door open without being pushy. Under 100 words. Sign as [Your Name].

Client Retention Prompts

Mid-Week Check-In Write a mid-week check-in message for my coaching client [Name]. They committed to [specific action] from our last session. Ask how it's going, offer one brief reframe if they hit resistance, and remind them of their bigger goal. Warm and brief — under 80 words.

Breakthrough Celebration Write a message celebrating a breakthrough for my coaching client [Name] who just [achievement — e.g., had their first $10K month / quit their job / had a difficult conversation they'd been avoiding]. Make them feel seen and proud. Connect it to the

work we've been doing together. Under 100 words. Program Launch Prompts Group Program Launch Email Write a launch email for my [program name] group coaching program. It's for [target audience] who want to [outcome]. Starts [date]. Investment: $[amount]. Limited to [X]

people. Lead with the transformation, not the features. Include 2 compelling bullet points about what's included. End with a clear CTA. Under 250 words. Instagram Content Post Write 3 Instagram captions for a [life/business] coach. Topics: the real reason most

people don't achieve their goals (it's not motivation), one question that changed everything for my clients, and what working with a coach actually looks like
