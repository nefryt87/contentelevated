SECTION 2: PATCH TEST PROTOCOL

When to Patch Test

✓ ALL new clients — before their first ever lash extension appointment

✓ Clients who have not had lash extensions in more than 6 months

✓ Clients who have had a recent change in medication or health status

✓ Clients with a known history of sensitive skin or allergies

✓ Clients switching to a new adhesive type

✓ [Patch testing protects your clients and protects your business. A sensitivity

reaction after a full set is preventable — a patch test takes 15 minutes.] Patch Test Process

✓ Step 1: Apply 5-10 lash extensions to the outer corner of one eye

✓ Step 2: Document the date, adhesive brand/batch number, and client name in your

records

✓ Step 3: Advise the client to monitor for redness, itching, swelling, or irritation over

the next 24-48 hours

✓ Step 4: Ask them to contact you immediately if they notice any reaction

✓ Step 5: If no reaction occurs: proceed with the full appointment

✓ Step 6: If a reaction occurs: remove extensions, do not rebook for lash extensions,

recommend the client see a GP if reaction is significant

✓ WHAT TO TELL THE CLIENT:

✓ 'We are applying a small number of lashes to check that your skin and eyes are

happy with the adhesive. This is a routine step I do with all new clients — it takes about 15 minutes and I will follow up with you in 24-48 hours.'

SECTION 3: LASH MAPPING GUIDE

Choosing the Right Lash Map for the Eye

EYE SHAPE RECOMMENDED MAP AVOID

Almond / balanced Most maps work beautifully — cat eye, doll, natural all suit this shape Nothing particularly to avoid Round Cat eye or elongated map — adds length to outer corner to create oval illusion Avoid doll eye / heavy centre weight — emphasises roundness

Hooded Longer lengths in centre/outer, avoid very long inner corner Avoid very dramatic curl on hooded lid — lashes touch the lid Monolid Open eye or doll map works well — lifts the eye Avoid very thick lashes close to the lash line

Deep set L curl or L+ — creates visible lift out of the socket Avoid heavy weight that sits inside the socket Wide set Emphasise inner corner — longer inner lashes to bring eyes closer Avoid heavy outer corner emphasis Close set Emphasise outer corner — longer towards outer edge

Avoid heavy inner corner emphasis Upturned Natural map or slight cat eye — match the natural lift Avoid very dramatic cat eye — can exaggerate the upturn Downturned Cat eye or lifted outer corner map — lifts the outer corner Avoid doll or natural maps that

follow the natural downward line
