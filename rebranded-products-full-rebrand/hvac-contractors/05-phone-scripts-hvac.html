Convert inbound calls, leave voicemails that get returned, handle objections, respond to reviews

SECTION 1: INBOUND CALL SCRIPTS

Standard Service Call

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — STANDARD SERVICE CALL

DISPATCHER: Thank you for calling [Company Name], this is [Name] — how can I help you today? [Listen fully. Let them describe the problem without interrupting.] DISPATCHER: Got it — that's something we can definitely take care of. Let me get a couple of quick details.

[Collect: full name, address, best callback number, problem description, urgency level] DISPATCHER: We have availability [today/tomorrow]. Our techs are licensed and insured — they'll call 30 minutes before arrival. Does [time window] work?

[Confirm. Read back the address. Always.] DISPATCHER: Perfect — you're all set. Anything else before I let you go? [KEY: A calm professional dispatcher builds trust before the tech ever arrives.] Emergency No-Heat / No-Cool Call

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — EMERGENCY CALL

DISPATCHER: [Company Name] — sounds like an emergency. Let's get this sorted quickly. [Get address immediately. Time matters.] DISPATCHER: I'm reaching our nearest available tech right now. Best callback number for the next 10 minutes?

DISPATCHER: While you wait — if you have pets or elderly family, move them to the most comfortable area. [Dispatch tech. Call customer back within 10 minutes with confirmed ETA.] DISPATCHER: Great — [Tech Name] is heading your way and will be there by [time].

[Always call back with a specific ETA. 'We sent someone' is not enough on an emergency call.] Price-Shopping Call — How Much Does It Cost?

SECTION 2: VOICEMAIL SCRIPTS

Estimate Follow-Up Voicemail

VOICEMAIL — ESTIMATE FOLLOW-UP

Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company Name] — just following up on the estimate for [job type] from [day]. Wanted to make sure you got it and see if you had any questions. We're available [timeframe] if you want to move forward. Call me at [Phone] —

that's [repeat number]. No pressure at all. [Keep voicemails under 25 seconds. Name, company, reason, number twice. That's it.] Seasonal Tune-Up Voicemail

SECTION 3: OBJECTION HANDLING

Objection: It's Too Expensive

OBJECTION — TOO EXPENSIVE

TECH: I hear you — more than you were hoping for. Can I ask what budget you were working with? I want to see if there's a way to make this work. TECH: We offer financing — you could spread this over [X] months at low or zero interest. Works out to about $[monthly amount] a month.
