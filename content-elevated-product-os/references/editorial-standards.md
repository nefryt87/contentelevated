# Editorial Standards

## Brand Voice

Premium, modern, useful, direct, and niche-specific. Avoid generic AI product language. The buyer should feel the product was built for their actual business.

## Rules

- Do not invent fake customer results unless clearly marked as examples.
- Do not use vague claims like "skyrocket your growth."
- Do not overuse "premium" inside product files.
- Do not include internal production language.
- Avoid generic "service business" wording when the niche is known.
- Every prompt should be immediately usable.
- Every template should include enough context to customize.
- Every document should deliver on its title.

## Proofread Passes

1. Mechanical: spelling, grammar, punctuation, duplicated headings, broken extraction.
2. Niche fit: language, buyer objections, services, outcomes.
3. Utility: can the buyer use this today?
4. Sales fit: does the product support the website and Payhip promise?
5. Final QA: title, sections, layout density, file naming.
