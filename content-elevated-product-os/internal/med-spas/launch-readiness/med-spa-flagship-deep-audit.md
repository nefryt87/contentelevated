## Design Review

The old direction leaned too much into generic beauty/luxury and fragmented extraction cards. The new direction is more appropriate for a med spa buyer:

- Calm clinical premium instead of heavy glam.
- More white space and clearer page hierarchy.
- Softer greens, ivory, blush, and deep teal rather than fake-luxury gold.
- CTA pages remain elevated but less dark/heavy.
- Pages use fewer cards and clearer headings to reduce PDF export risk.

## Content Review

The strongest buyer value is now:

- First-time client anxiety reduction through pre-treatment education.
- Inquiry-to-consult conversion through clearer replies.
- Post-treatment check-ins and maintenance reminders.
- Privacy-aware review response rules.
- Consent-aware before/after and testimonial guidance.
- A 90-day calendar organized around education, conversion, retention, and referrals.

## Compliance Guardrails Added

This bundle should remain marketing/client communication support, not clinical advice. The new source includes language reminding buyers to review treatment-specific claims, prep instructions, aftercare, and policies through a qualified provider.

Sources used for direction:

- FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance: advertising must be truthful, not misleading, and substantiated, including implied claims and net impression.
- HHS HIPAA Privacy Rule: protected health information has privacy safeguards and public/client communications should avoid disclosing identifiable health information.
