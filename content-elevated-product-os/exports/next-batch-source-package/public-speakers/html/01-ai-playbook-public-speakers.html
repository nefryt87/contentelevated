Your Speaker One-Sheet A one-sheet is a single-page PDF summary of your speaking offer — your topic(s), key outcomes, social proof, and booking contact. It is the most important document a professional speaker has and most speakers have one that is either outdated or generic.

Speaker asset

Prompt — Speaker One-Sheet Copy

Write the copy for my professional speaker one-sheet. My name: [Name]. My speaking topic/niche: [topic]. My signature talk title: [title]. The 3 key outcomes audiences leave with: [outcomes]. My background credibility: [relevant credentials, experience, notable past engagements]. 3 testimonials or quotes: [paste quotes if available]. My speaking style in 2 sentences: [describe your style — e.g., 'data- driven and story-led' / 'high energy and immediately practical']. I want the copy to: be punchy and skimmable, communicate value to event organizers, and end with a clear booking CTA. Under 400 words total.

Speaker asset

Prompt — Speaker Bio (3 Lengths)

Write three versions of my professional speaker bio: (1) long form (200 words) for event programmes and websites, (2) medium form (100 words) for event introductions, (3) short form (50 words) for social media and slides. My name: [Name]. My speaking topic: [topic]. Key credentials: [your background — experience, titles, notable achievements]. Signature talk or specialty: [describe]. Style and approach: [2-3 words]. Written in third person. Professional and authoritative.

Chapter 4: Linkedin & Content Strategy

LinkedIn as Your Primary Booking Channel LinkedIn is the highest-ROI content platform for professional speakers. The speakers who post consistently on LinkedIn fill their calendars with inbound inquiries.
