emergency.

The Formula Behind It

Price Floor = Supply Cost + (Total Minutes ÷ 60) × (Hourly Rate + Overhead Per Hour)

The calculator outputs three numbers for every service:

Price Floor — the absolute minimum you should charge (covers costs + hits your target rate)

Recommended Price — price floor + 20% margin buffer for slow weeks, product waste, and

unbillable time

Margin at Current Price — if you enter your current price, it shows exactly what you're keeping

per service

Break-Even Tracker

Your break-even point is the number of services you need to perform each month just to cover

your fixed costs. Anything above that is profit. Most salon owners have no idea what this number is.

If self-employed, include your monthly premium

Unexpected costs — recommend 10% of total above

The Formula

Break-Even Services = Total Monthly Fixed Costs ÷ Average Revenue Per Service

The tracker outputs:

Break-even service count — how many clients you need per month just to cover costs

Break-even per week — broken down to weekly targets for easier planning

Profit per additional service — every client above break-even earns this amount

Days to break-even — based on your average daily client count

Use this number every month. It changes when your rent goes up, when you add a software tool, or

when your average ticket changes. Check it quarterly at minimum.

The 3 Levers

Every income goal can be reached by pulling one or more of these levers. The calculator shows you

the math for all three:

Raise your average ticket — add retail, upsell add-ons, or adjust service pricing

See more clients — fill gaps in your schedule, reduce no-shows, improve rebooking rate

Work more days — add a day or extend hours if capacity is the constraint

Most stylists find lever 1 (raising average ticket) is the fastest path. A $20 increase across 20 clients

per week is $400/week — $20,000/year — with zero additional time in the chair.
