Prompts 01-03

Patient flow prompts

Turn common gaps into polished touchpoints.

These prompts are designed for high-friction moments where patients need clarity, reassurance, and an easy next step.

01

New Patient Inquiry Reply

Create a warm response for a new patient asking about a service, insurance, or first appointment.

Write a friendly reply to a new dental patient asking about [service/question]. Include a warm welcome, a clear next step, what they can expect, and a booking link placeholder. Keep it under 120 words.
02

Appointment Reminder Sequence

Help reduce confusion with a 48-hour reminder, morning-of message, and no-show recovery note.

Write a 3-message reminder sequence for patient [Name] with an appointment on [date] at [time]. Message 1: confirm and prepare. Message 2: quick arrival reminder. Message 3: warm no-show recovery with rebooking link. Each under 60 words.
03

Treatment Plan Follow-Up

Support patients who left with a recommendation but have not scheduled the next step yet.

Write a treatment plan follow-up email for patient [Name] who was recommended [treatment] on [date]. Explain why timing matters, answer one common hesitation, and invite them to schedule. Caring, professional, under 150 words.
04

Customization Tip

For every prompt, add the treatment type, tone, booking link, patient stage, and any required practice-specific disclaimers.

Prompts 04-05

Retention and trust

Bring patients back with better communication.

Use these prompts to keep the relationship warm after the visit and make returning to care easier.

04

Review Request Message

Ask happy patients for a review in a way that feels natural, grateful, and easy to act on.

Write a Google review request message for patient [Name] who recently completed [visit/treatment] and had a positive experience. Keep it warm, specific, and under 80 words. Include [REVIEW LINK].
05

Patient Reactivation Email

Reach patients who have not been seen recently with a gentle, non-guilt-based reminder.

Write a patient reactivation email for [Practice Name]. Patient [Name] has not visited since [date]. Mention they may be due for [cleaning/treatment/checkup], keep the tone kind, and include [BOOKING LINK]. Under 120 words.
06
