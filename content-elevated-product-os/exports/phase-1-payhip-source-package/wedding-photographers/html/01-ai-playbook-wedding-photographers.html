Introduction

The wedding photographers booking premium couples consistently aren't just the most talented shooters. They respond to inquiries within 2 hours. They follow up on ghosted leads with the right message. They maintain venue and planner relationships that send warm referrals year-round. And they deliver galleries with an experience that makes couples feel celebrated rather than just served. This playbook gives you 7 chapters covering every scenario where AI saves you time and wins you more bookings — with copy-paste prompts throughout.

03

Studio asset

Conversion

The 2-Hour Response Window Couples often contact 5-8 photographers simultaneously. The first professional, warm, and specific response wins a disproportionate number of consultations. Your response should feel like it was written specifically for them — because with AI, it takes 3 minutes to make it so.

04

Studio asset

Prompt — Same-Day Inquiry Response

Write a warm, enthusiastic inquiry response email for my wedding photography studio. The couple [Names] reached out about their wedding on [date] at [venue]. They mentioned: [paste 2-3 details from their inquiry — venue style, vibe, what matters to them]. My photography style is [your style — e.g., romantic documentary / bright and airy / moody film]. I want to: (1) make them feel like I genuinely read their inquiry, (2) briefly describe how I'd approach their specific wedding, (3) invite them to a discovery call with a specific time offered. Warm, excited, under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

Prompt — 3-Touch Follow-Up Sequence

Write a 3-message follow-up sequence for a wedding photography inquiry that hasn't responded. Couple [Names], wedding [date] at [venue]. Message 1 (Day 3): warm check-in, add one piece of relevant value about their venue or wedding style. Message 2 (Day 8): share a relevant before/after or portfolio piece with a brief note. Message 3 (Day 14): soft close — 'closing my inquiry folder for [their month]' — leaves door open. Each under 80 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

The Post-Booking Welcome System The experience between booking and the wedding is where most photographers lose referrals before the wedding even happens. A thoughtful onboarding sequence sets expectations, builds excitement, and positions you as someone who is genuinely invested in their day.

Presenting Your Pricing Confidently Photographers who struggle with pricing conversations almost always do so because they present the number without enough context. Price is only uncomfortable when value hasn't been established first.

Prompt — Pricing Email Response

Write an email response to a couple who asked 'what are your packages and prices?' for wedding photography. My packages start at $[amount] and go to $ [amount]. Rather than just listing prices, I want to: (1) briefly contextualize what affects wedding photography investment, (2) describe what's included at each level, (3) invite them to a call to discuss what fits their day. Confident, not apologetic about the price. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name]. Handling the Budget Objection
