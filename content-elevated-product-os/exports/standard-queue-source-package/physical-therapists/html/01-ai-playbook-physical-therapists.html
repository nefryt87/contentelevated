Chapter 3: Reducing No-Shows And Protecting

Chapter 4: Patient Retention — Completing

Plans of Care The average PT patient completes only 60–70% of their prescribed plan of care. The gap between prescribed and completed visits represents both revenue loss and suboptimal patient outcomes. Communication is the most powerful lever for improving both. • The Between-Visit Check-In: A short message 2–3 days after each visit asking about soreness, pain levels, and HEP adherence. Takes 90 seconds with AI. Patients feel supported between appointments. Drop-off rates fall significantly. • The Plateau Acknowledgment: When a patient's progress slows, most stop coming without saying anything.

Chapter 5: Home Exercise Program

Communication That Actually Works HEP adherence is one of the most significant predictors of PT outcomes — and one of the most commonly poor. The difference between patients who do their exercises and those who don't is almost always communication and understanding. AI makes HEP communication systematic and effortless. • The HEP Companion Email: Every time a HEP is issued or updated, AI writes a companion email that explains each exercise in plain language, describes the common mistakes to avoid, explains why each exercise matters for their specific condition, and provides encouragement.
