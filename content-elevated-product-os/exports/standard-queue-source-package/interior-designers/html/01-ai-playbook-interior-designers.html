Chapter 2: Proposal & Fee Presentation

How you present your fee is as important as what it is. Designers who lose projects on price rarely lose because the budget isn't there — they lose because the value wasn't communicated clearly enough before the number landed. The Pre-Proposal Value Builder Before you ever send a proposal number, your client should already understand: what they're paying for, why your fee reflects the scope, and what happens if they try to do it without you. This is done in the discovery call and the post-call follow-up — not in the proposal itself.

Partnership System

One strong realtor relationship maintained well can send 3-8 projects per year. The designers with the most consistent project pipelines have 5-10 active referral partners — not just satisfied past clients. The Monthly Value Touch The difference between a realtor who refers once and one who refers consistently is a monthly value touch. Not a sales message — a genuinely useful update about design trends, staging tips, or renovation ROI that makes them look smart in front of their clients.
