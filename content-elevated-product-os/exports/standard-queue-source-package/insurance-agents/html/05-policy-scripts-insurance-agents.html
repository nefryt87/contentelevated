Objection — Can't Afford It

YOU: I hear you — and I want to respect that. Can I ask one question: what would happen financially to your family / business / assets if something happened to you tomorrow and you didn't have coverage? [Let them answer. Don't jump in.] YOU: The reason I ask is that can't afford insurance and can't afford to be uninsured often mean very different things when you actually run the numbers. YOU: Let me put together the most cost-effective option for your situation — sometimes people are genuinely surprised how manageable the monthly cost is for meaningful coverage. Can I do that and send it over? Objection: Need to Talk to My Spouse

03

Objection — Need To Talk To Spouse

YOU: Of course — this absolutely should be a joint decision. Would it be helpful if I put together a one-page summary you could share with them before you discuss it? YOU: Or even better — would your spouse have 15 minutes to join a quick call so I can answer both your questions at once? Joint calls are usually much more efficient. [Always offer a concrete next step. 'Talk to your spouse' without a next step usually means the conversation dies.]
