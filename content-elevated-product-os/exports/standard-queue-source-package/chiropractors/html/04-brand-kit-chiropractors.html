Section 1: Practice Positioning

Patients choose a chiropractor based on trust, proximity, and reputation — in that order. Your brand is what builds trust before they ever call. A clear, consistent, professional visual identity signals that you take your practice as seriously as you take patient care. Your Practice Promise We help [patient type — e.g., active adults / families / desk workers / athletes] [achieve result — e.g., get out of pain and stay that way / move better and feel stronger / recover from injury without surgery] through [your approach — e.g., gentle, evidence-based chiropractic care / personalized treatment plans / whole- body wellness].

Section 5: Photo & Video Guide

Practice Photography Tips • Clinic photos: Clean, bright, uncluttered — patients are evaluating whether they'll feel comfortable • Team photos: Approachable smiles, professional but not stiff — white coat optional depending on brand • Action shots: Treatment in progress (with patient consent) convert well — shows patients what to expect • Lighting: Natural light from windows or a ring light — avoid harsh overhead fluorescents
