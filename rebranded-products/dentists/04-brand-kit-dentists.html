DENTAL PRACTICE

BRAND KIT & CANVA TEMPLATE GUIDE

Build a practice brand that makes patients feel safe before they arrive. Color palettes • Fonts • Canva templates • Video guide • 7-day launch checklist

SECTION 1: PRACTICE POSITIONING

Dental anxiety affects 36% of the population. Your brand — before patients ever walk through the door — either amplifies that anxiety or reduces it. The most successful dental brands feel warm, modern, and trustworthy rather than clinical and cold. Every

visual and messaging choice should reinforce: this is a safe, comfortable place. Your Practice Promise We help [patient type — e.g., families / adults with dental anxiety / busy professionals] [achieve outcome — e.g., maintain a healthy smile for life / finally

feel comfortable at the dentist / get the smile they've always wanted] in a [your approach — e.g., gentle, judgment-free environment / modern, comfortable practice / caring family atmosphere]. Example: 'We help families in [City] keep their smiles healthy for life in a warm,

judgment-free practice where everyone — especially nervous patients — feels completely at ease.'

Brand Personality (Pick 2–3)

• Gentle — You meet patients where they are, especially anxious ones

• Trustworthy — You explain everything and never recommend unnecessary

treatment

• Modern — Your practice is up to date with technology and technique

• Family — You treat patients like people you genuinely care about

• Expert — Your team is highly trained and stays current with continuing education

SECTION 3: TYPOGRAPHY

USE FONT NOTES

Headlines Libre Baskerville Bold Elegant and authoritative — conveys clinical expertise without feeling cold. Free on Google Fonts. Subheadings Lato SemiBold Clean and modern — bridges clinical authority with approachability.

Body Copy Lato Regular Highly readable across all sizes — excellent for patient-facing communications. CTA / Callouts Lato Bold Used for appointment CTAs, key statistics, and urgent messages only.

• Hire a local photographer for a half-day shoot — worth every dollar for the

impression it creates

• Refresh photos every 2–3 years — outdated photos signal a stagnant practice
