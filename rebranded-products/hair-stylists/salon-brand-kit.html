Section 1

Brand Foundation

Your brand foundation is the strategic layer that everything else is built on. Get this right and your voice, visuals, and content will feel coherent naturally. Step 1 — Your Positioning Statement

A positioning statement is one or two sentences that capture who you serve, what you do, and what makes you different. It is not your tagline — it is the internal compass you use when making

brand decisions.

The Formula: I help [target client] achieve [desired outcome] through [your approach/differentiator] — without [common pain point they want to avoid]. Example (fill this in for your own salon):

I help busy women in their 30s and 40s get low-maintenance color that grows out beautifully — through techniques that prioritize the health of their hair, without the commitment of 6-week touch-ups. Step 2 — Your Target Client Profile

Section 2

Brand Voice

Your brand voice is how your business sounds when it speaks. It is consistent across every caption, text, email, and response to a review. Clients who love your voice will come looking for it — and it is one of the hardest things for competitors to copy.

Step 1 — Choose Your 3 Voice Attributes Pick 3 adjectives from the list below that describe how you want your brand to sound. For each one, add what it means in practice and what it does NOT mean:

Warm · Direct · Expert · Playful · Confident · Nurturing · Edgy · Premium · Approachable · Educational · Empowering · Honest · Witty · Calm · Energetic Voice Attribute In Practice This Means... This Does NOT Mean... Example: Warm

Lighting: Natural light or warm artificial? Bright and airy or moody and dramatic? Background: Clean white/cream, salon setting, outdoor, or dark backdrop? Color grading: Warm tones, cool tones, desaturated film look, or true-to-life color?

Client styling: Styled and polished, or natural/undone — which fits your brand?

Bio & Profile Copy Your bio is the first thing a potential client reads. It needs to communicate who you are, who you serve, and what to do next — in under 150 words. These templates give you a framework to adapt. Instagram Bio (150 characters maximum)

Format: What you do | Who you do it for | Location | Call to action (link in bio) Specialist approach: Balayage specialist | Natural, lived-in color for low-maintenance women | [City] | Book below ↓ Full-service studio:

Hair color + cuts for [client type] | [City], [State] | [X] years behind the chair | New clients welcome ↓ Personality-forward: [Your specialty] by appointment | I make [client type] feel like their best selves | [City] | Book your seat ↓

Google Business Profile Description (150–250 words) Structure: Who you are → What you specialize in → Who your ideal client is → What makes your experience different → How to book.

[SALON NAME] is a [hair salon / suite / independent stylist] based in [CITY, STATE] specializing in [TOP 1-2 SERVICES]. We work with [CLIENT DESCRIPTION: e.g., women who want low-maintenance color

that looks expensive, clients with textured hair who want healthy natural styles, brides and wedding parties] — and we take the time to do it right. [WHAT MAKES YOU DIFFERENT: e.g., Every appointment

starts with a thorough consultation because we believe the best results come from truly understanding what you want and what your hair can realistically achieve.] [ADDITIONAL DIFFERENTIATOR: e.g., We use only professional-grade color lines and invest in continuing education

every year to bring the latest techniques to our clients.] [CLOSING CTA: Book your appointment online or give us a call — we would love to meet you.]

Booking Page / Website About Section (100–150 words)
