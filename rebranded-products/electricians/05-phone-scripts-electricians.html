SECTION 1: INBOUND CALL SCRIPTS

Standard Residential Service Call

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — STANDARD RESIDENTIAL CALL

DISPATCHER: Thank you for calling [Company Name], this is [Name] — how can I help you today? [Listen fully. Let them describe the problem without interrupting. Don't diagnose on the phone.] DISPATCHER: Got it — that's something we can definitely take care of. Let me get a

couple of quick details. [Collect: full name, address, best callback number, description of the issue, urgency level] DISPATCHER: We have availability [today / tomorrow / this week]. Our electricians

are licensed and insured — they'll call you 30 minutes before arrival. Does [time window] work? [Confirm. Read the address back. Always.] DISPATCHER: Perfect — you're all set. Any questions before I let you go? [KEY: Collect all details before quoting anything. Never give a

firm price over the phone for diagnostic work.] Emergency Call — Burning Smell / Sparking / Power Out

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — ELECTRICAL EMERGENCY

DISPATCHER: [Company Name] — sounds like an emergency. I want to help you quickly. What's happening? [Listen. Assess safety risk immediately.] DISPATCHER: First — if you can smell burning or see sparking, please turn off the

main breaker at your electrical panel right now if it's safe to do so. Do you know where your panel is? [Guide them through this if needed. Do NOT let them stay near an active electrical hazard.] DISPATCHER: I'm reaching out to our nearest licensed electrician right now. What's

your address and best callback number? [Dispatch. Call back within 10 minutes with a confirmed ETA.] DISPATCHER: [Your Name] is heading to you now and will be there by [time]. If anything changes before they arrive, call me directly at [Phone].

[If burning smell or smoke: 'If the burning smell continues after you turn off the breaker, please call 911 and evacuate. Electrical fires can start inside walls.'] Price-Shopping Call — 'How Much Does It Cost?'

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — PRICE SHOPPING

CUSTOMER: How much does it cost to [rewire an outlet / install a panel / run a new circuit]? DISPATCHER: Great question — and I want to give you a real number, not a guess. For [panel upgrades], most residential jobs run between $[X] and $[X] depending on

the size of your panel and the condition of your existing wiring. For smaller jobs like outlets and circuits, our service call is $[amount] and our electrician will give you an exact price before any work starts.

DISPATCHER: Can I get you scheduled for a free assessment? It's the only way to give you an accurate number — and there's no obligation after the assessment. [Give ranges for standard jobs. Never give firm prices for diagnostic work. Always offer the free assessment as the next

step.]

SECTION 3: OBJECTION HANDLING SCRIPTS

Objection: "IT'S TOO EXPENSIVE"

OBJECTION SCRIPT — IT'S TOO EXPENSIVE

ELEC: I hear you — it's more than you were expecting. Can I ask: is it the total number, or a question about what's included? [If total number:] ELEC: We do offer [payment plan / financing for jobs over $X] if spreading the cost

out would help. For a job like this, that works out to about $[monthly amount] a

month.

[If questioning what's included:] ELEC: Let me quickly walk you through what our quote covers that a lower bid might not: we're licensed, we pull the permit where required, our work is inspected, and everything comes with a [X]-year workmanship guarantee. A lower quote may not

include all of those — and when something goes wrong, you'll want those protections. [Never apologize for your price. State it, explain the value, hold it.] Objection: "I GOT A CHEAPER QUOTE"
