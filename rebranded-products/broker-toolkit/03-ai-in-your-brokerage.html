Renewal Reminder Email Write a friendly, professional email to a small business client reminding them that their group health plan renews in [X weeks]. Their company is [COMPANY NAME] with [# employees]. Mention rates are going

up [X]% industry-wide and I'd like to schedule a 20-minute review call to explore options. Tone: warm, not alarmist. Sign off as [YOUR NAME].

Plan Comparison Explainer

Write a plain-English explanation comparing an HMO, PPO, and HDHP for a business owner with [# employees] who are mostly [age range]. Budget is tight. Focus on what matters most to a small employer. Keep it under 200 words.

Follow-Up After No Response

Write a brief, non-pushy follow-up email to [CLIENT NAME] who I met with [X weeks] ago about their health benefits. They haven't responded. Reference our conversation about [TOPIC]. End with a soft CTA. Keep it under 5 sentences.

LINKEDIN CONTENT

Thought Leadership Post Write a LinkedIn post from the perspective of an independent health insurance broker. Topic: [INSERT TOPIC]. Tone: conversational, expert, not salesy. Include a question at the end to drive comments. 150–200 words.

Client Win Story (Anonymous)

Write a LinkedIn case study post about a client who saved money or improved their benefits. Details: [INDUSTRY], [# employees], [PROBLEM], [SOLUTION], [RESULT]. Use first-person broker perspective. Start with a hook line. ~180 words.

YOUR AI-POWERED WEEKLY WORKFLOW

MONDAY

 Use Claude to draft 3 LinkedIn posts for the week (15 min)  Review HubSpot CRM — any renewals or follow-ups due this week?  Check Otter.ai for unreviewed meeting notes from last week

TUE – THU

AI COMPLIANCE GUARDRAILS

IMPORTANT: AI tools are assistants, not advisors. Always review AI-generated content before sending. Never input client names, SSNs, health information, or PHI/PII into AI tools unless using a HIPAA-compliant platform with a signed BAA. ! Never input PHI into free AI tools

Use generic placeholders like [CLIENT] when drafting. Real names + health data belong in your secure CRM only. ! Always review AI-generated advice AI can be confidently wrong about carrier rules, state regs, or ACA specifics. Use it to draft — not to advise. !
