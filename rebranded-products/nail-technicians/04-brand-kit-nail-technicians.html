WHY YOUR BRAND ATTRACTS YOUR IDEAL

CLIENT

Nail clients choose their tech based on Instagram before they ever DM. Your brand — your palette, your typography, how your feed grid looks — tells a potential client within 5 seconds whether you are the right fit for them. A distinctive, consistent visual identity

attracts the clients who value your work, tip well, and come back every 2-3 weeks. This kit gives you two palettes, typography pairings, and Canva strategies built specifically for nail technicians.

8 CANVA TEMPLATE STRATEGIES

Template 1: Nail Art Reveal Post

Search 'nail art Instagram post' or 'beauty close-up template'. A close-up nail photo fills the frame. Minimal text overlay: your studio name small at the bottom. Keep it clean — the nail art is the hero. Create 10 of these with consistent overlay style and your grid will

look immediately professional and cohesive.

Template 2: Before & After Transformation

Search 'before after beauty post' or 'nail transformation Instagram'. Two-panel layout. Left: nails before (grown out, damaged, plain). Right: fresh set after. Your studio name bottom corner. With client permission. These generate more DMs than any other post type for nail techs.

Template 3: Seasonal Campaign Graphic

Search 'seasonal beauty announcement' or 'nail art campaign post'. Bold, colourful. '[Season] Nails Are Here' in your largest heading font. Preview of 2-3 designs. Booking info. Post 1-2 weeks before the season and update monthly. These drive more bookings

than any other single post type.

Template 4: Instagram Story — Booking Open

Search 'booking available story' or 'appointment open Instagram story'. Vertical. Your best nail art photo as background. Semi-transparent overlay. 'BOOKING [MONTH]' in bold. Your DM or link. Post every time you open new slots — at least weekly during peak season.

Template 5: TikTok / Reel Cover Frame

Search 'TikTok thumbnail beauty' or 'Reel cover template'. For the first frame of every video: your key message in large text ('Watch me do this nail art', 'Gel vs acrylic — the truth'). Your studio name small at bottom. Brand colour background. Makes people click

instead of scroll past.

Template 6: Nail Care Education Graphic

Search 'beauty tip infographic' or 'nail care tips post'. '5 Tips for Longer Lasting Gel' or 'Nail Health Guide'. One tip per carousel slide or all 5 on one graphic. Your name and

booking link. These save at very high rates — clients bookmark them and share them

with friends.

Template 7: Price List / Service Menu

Search 'beauty price list Instagram' or 'salon services menu'. Clean, organised layout: service name, brief description, price. Your studio name at top. Style and aesthetic consistent with your palette. Share in stories, feed, and booking highlights. Reduces

Template 8: Instagram Highlight Covers

Search 'highlight cover nails' or 'nail studio Instagram icons'. Create 8 round covers: Portfolio, Reviews, Seasonal, Booking, Aftercare, Price List, About Me, Press-Ons (if applicable). Consistent palette. Your profile looks immediately professional and organised.
