SECTION 2: NAIL HEALTH ASSESSMENT GUIDE

What to Look For Before Starting a Service

CONDITION WHAT IT MEANS ACTION

Fungal infection (yellow, thick, crumbling nail) Contagious — do not apply product over Refer to a GP. Do not proceed. Active skin infection / open wounds around nail Risk of cross-contamination Do not proceed in affected area Severely bitten / damaged nails

Weak natural nail plate Advise on nail strengthening before extensions; adjust service Very thin / peeling nails after previous product Over-processing or product allergy Apply lighter product; prioritise nail health over length

Lifting on old product Air or moisture under product Full removal before new application — do not fill over lifting Discolouration under product Possible bacterial or fungal concern Remove product; assess; refer if unclear Allergic reaction signs (redness, itching, swelling)

Product sensitivity or allergy Stop service; do not apply product; note allergy in file Cuticle inflammation / paronychia Possible infection Do not cut inflamed cuticles; advise client to see a GP This guide is a general reference. Always follow the requirements of your nail

technician licence and jurisdiction, and refer clients to a medical professional when you are uncertain about a nail or skin condition.
