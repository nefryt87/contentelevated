FLORIST

The AI Playbook for Florists & Floral Designers Convert more inquiries, present proposals with confidence, build wedding planner and venue partnerships, and grow a studio that runs on referrals

INTRODUCTION

The florists building thriving studios aren't just the most creative with blooms. They respond to every inquiry the same day with a message that makes the client feel heard and excited. They present proposals that communicate the transformative power of

flowers before they present a number. They maintain active relationships with wedding planners, venue coordinators, and photographers who send warm referrals year-round. They post consistently on Instagram and Pinterest with content that attracts their ideal

client. And they create a client experience so consistently beautiful that every

arrangement becomes a referral. This playbook covers every high-leverage scenario — from weddings to corporate accounts to weekly studio subscriptions — with copy-paste AI prompts throughout.

CHAPTER 1: INQUIRY RESPONSE & QUOTE

CONVERSION

The Same-Day Inquiry Response System Brides and event clients often contact 3-5 florists simultaneously. The first response that feels personal, visually knowledgeable, and enthusiastic wins a disproportionate number of consultations.

PROMPT — WEDDING FLORAL INQUIRY RESPONSE

Write a warm, enthusiastic inquiry response email for my floral studio. The bride [Name] reached out about wedding florals for her wedding on [date] at [venue]. She mentioned: [paste details — style she described, flowers she loves, any mood board

details, budget indication]. My studio speciality and aesthetic: [your style — e.g., romantic garden / architectural modern / wild meadow / tropical lush]. I want to: (1) make her feel like I genuinely read her inquiry and understand her vision, (2) briefly

describe how I'd interpret her style, (3) mention what a consultation looks like, (4) invite her to book a call or consultation. Warm and evocative, under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

PROMPT — EVENT / CORPORATE INQUIRY RESPONSE

Write a professional inquiry response email for my floral studio. Client [Name] at [Company] reached out about florals for [event type — e.g., corporate gala / product launch / private party]. They mentioned: [details — scale, aesthetic, date, venue]. I

want to: (1) show I understand the brand or event aesthetic, (2) briefly describe how I'd approach the brief, (3) ask 1-2 clarifying questions, (4) invite them to a design consultation. Professional and creative. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

The Budget Conversation The budget conversation is where many floral inquiries stall. Florists who handle it proactively — with education and honesty about what different investment levels produce — convert at significantly higher rates.

PROMPT — BUDGET CONVERSATION EMAIL

Write an email that tactfully addresses a potential wedding client who has shared a budget that may be lower than expected. Their budget: $[amount]. Their vision: [description]. I want to: (1) honour their vision genuinely, (2) honestly explain what

their budget can achieve and where the investment typically goes in wedding florals, (3) offer either a scaled-back version or a way to prioritise the most impactful arrangements. Educational and warm — never condescending. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

CHAPTER 3: WEDDING CLIENT EXPERIENCE

The Consultation to Installation Experience Weddings are the highest-value and highest-visibility floristry work. The client experience from first consultation to installation day determines both the outcome and the referrals that follow.

PROMPT — POST-CONSULTATION PROPOSAL FOLLOW-UP

Write a post-consultation proposal follow-up email for my floral studio. Client [Name], consultation was [date]. I'm proposing a floral package for their [date] wedding at [venue] — approximate investment $[amount]. I want to: (1) recap the

vision we discussed in 2-3 evocative sentences, (2) briefly summarise the key arrangements included, (3) present the investment and next step. Evocative and confident. Under 250 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

CHAPTER 4: CORPORATE & SUBSCRIPTION

FLORISTRY

Building Corporate Accounts & Weekly Subscriptions Corporate floral accounts and weekly studio subscriptions provide predictable recurring revenue that stabilises the seasonal nature of wedding work. One corporate account

with weekly flowers can generate $2,000-$10,000+ per month.

PROMPT — CORPORATE ACCOUNT PROPOSAL

Write a corporate flower subscription proposal for my floral studio. Client: [Company name] at [office/hotel/restaurant]. I'm proposing weekly flower arrangements for [locations in their space — e.g., reception desk, boardroom, client meeting rooms].

Investment: $[amount] per week / month which covers [what's included — design, delivery, vase maintenance]. I want to: (1) describe the impact of beautiful flowers on an office or hospitality environment, (2) present the service clearly, (3) offer a trial

arrangement. Professional and compelling. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

PROMPT — RESIDENTIAL SUBSCRIPTION PROPOSAL

Write a weekly flower subscription proposal for my floral studio. Client [Name] is interested in a weekly or bi-weekly home flower subscription. Investment: $[amount] per week/fortnight which covers [what's included — seasonal arrangement, delivery,

vase rotation]. I want to: (1) describe what it feels like to always have fresh flowers at home, (2) outline the service clearly, (3) offer a trial. Warm and aspirational. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

Write 5 Instagram captions for a florist specialising in [your style]. Topics: (1) a recent wedding or event reveal — describe the flowers, the venue, the atmosphere (anonymise client), (2) a seasonal flower you're obsessed with right now and why,

CHAPTER 6: REVIEWS & REPUTATION

The Review Generation System

PROMPT — POST-WEDDING REVIEW REQUEST

Write a post-wedding review request email for my floral studio. Client [Name], wedding [date] at [venue]. Send 1-2 weeks after when photos are starting to come in. I want to: (1) check in on how the wedding went and express genuine joy for

them, (2) share one personal memory from installing or seeing the finished florals, (3) make a warm personal ask for a Google and/or WeddingWire review. Emotional and genuine. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].
