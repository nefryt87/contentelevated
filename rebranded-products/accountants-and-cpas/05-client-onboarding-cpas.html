CONVERSATION SCRIPTS

Introducing Tax Planning to a Compliance-Only Client

SCRIPT — ADVISORY SERVICES INTRODUCTION CONVERSATION

YOU: I want to raise something with you that I think could make a real difference to your tax bill — and I want to be direct about it because I think you're leaving money on the table. YOU: Everything we've done together has been compliance-focused — accurate

returns, filed on time. That's important. But there's another layer that most clients don't know to ask about: proactive planning. YOU: Planning means we look at your situation in [Q3/Q4 / mid-year], make decisions

about income timing, deductions, retirement contributions, entity structure — before December 31, when there's still something we can do. Right now we're mostly reporting what happened. YOU: Based on [specific thing about their situation — income level, business

structure, upcoming transaction], I think there are real opportunities. Would you be open to a planning conversation? [Frame it as serving their interests, not selling a service. You're a professional advisor who sees an opportunity to help — not a salesperson looking to upsell.]

When a Client Asks 'What Else Can You Do For Me?'

SCRIPT — FULL SERVICE DISCOVERY CONVERSATION

YOU: Great question — I'd love to walk you through what we offer beyond tax preparation. YOU: For [business owners], we can help with: bookkeeping and financial statement preparation, payroll, quarterly estimated tax planning, business structure

optimisation, and what I'd call CFO-lite advisory — being a sounding board when you're making big financial decisions. YOU: For [individuals], beyond the annual return, we can do: year-round tax planning, assistance with major transactions like real estate or business sales, IRS

representation if you ever receive a notice, and estate planning coordination alongside your attorney. YOU: The clients I have the most impact with are the ones I hear from more than once a year. What matters most to you right now?

[Let them tell you what they need. Then match your services to their stated priorities.]

IRS Notice Triage — What to Tell Clients Immediately

✓ CP2000 — Underreporter inquiry: IRS matching return data. Common, not an audit.

Respond within 60 days.

✓ CP503 / CP504 — Balance due notices: Taxes owed. Address promptly to avoid

lien.

✓ CP90 / 1058 — Intent to levy: Urgent. Contact client immediately.

✓ Letter 3219 — Notice of deficiency: 90-day letter. Do not ignore.

✓ Letter 525 / 531 — Audit examination: Respond through proper channels.

✓ CP01 / CP01A — Identity protection: Identity theft or PIN issue.

✓ [Rule: Never let clients respond to IRS notices directly without speaking to you

first. The response matters as much as the issue.]

Client IRS Notice Response Protocol Email

SUBJECT You received an IRS notice — here's what to do, [First Name]

Dear [First Name], Thank you for forwarding the IRS notice. I've reviewed it and want to give you some immediate context so you're not worrying unnecessarily. What this notice is: [plain-language explanation of the specific notice type and what triggered it].

What it is NOT: [reassurance where appropriate — e.g., 'This is not an audit. It is a routine matching notice.'] What happens next:

- I will [specific action — draft response / review the discrepancy / request an

account transcript] by [date]

- Your response deadline: [date] — we have sufficient time

- I may need from you: [any specific information or documents]

Please do not contact the IRS directly — let me handle the response. This is exactly the type of situation where having a CPA in your corner matters. I'll update you by [date]. [Your Name] | [Firm Name] | [Phone]
