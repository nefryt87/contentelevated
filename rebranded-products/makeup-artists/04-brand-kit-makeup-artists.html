Build a beauty brand that books itself. Color palettes • Fonts • Canva templates • Niche framework • 7-day checklist

SECTION 1: BRAND POSITIONING

The most booked MUAs aren't necessarily the most skilled — they're the most clearly positioned and consistently visible. Your brand communicates who you are, who you serve, and why you're the only choice before a client ever reaches out. Your Beauty Brand Promise

I help [client type — e.g., brides / women of color / editorial models / prom queens] feel [emotional outcome — e.g., confident / timeless / bold / like the most beautiful version of themselves] through [your approach — e.g., luxurious, long-wear

makeup tailored to their features and vision]. Example: 'I help brides in [City] feel effortlessly beautiful on their wedding day — with a look they'll love in photos for the rest of their life.' Niche Options — The More Specific, The More Bookable

• Bridal and wedding party makeup

• Editorial and fashion/print

• Film, TV, and commercial

• Special occasion (galas, proms, events)

• Mature skin and age-positive beauty

• Melanin-rich and textured skin specialist

• Airbrush specialist

• Natural and clean beauty

• Bold, avant-garde, and creative looks

• On-location and destination weddings

SECTION 3: TYPOGRAPHY

USE FONT NOTES

Headlines (Romantic) Cormorant Garamond Bold Elegant and luxurious — perfect for bridal and high-end beauty brands. Free on Google Fonts. Headlines (Editorial) Josefin Sans Bold Clean and modern — ideal for fashion-forward and editorial MUAs.

Body Copy Lato Regular Readable and friendly — great for captions, emails, and print materials. CTA / Emphasis Lato Bold For booking buttons, key offers, and important callouts.
