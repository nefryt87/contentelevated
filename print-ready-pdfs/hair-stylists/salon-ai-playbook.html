AI for Marketing

Content, captions, email campaigns, and how to make AI sound like you — not a robot.

Training AI to Write in Your Voice The most common complaint about AI content is that it sounds generic. The fix is simple: you have to teach it your voice before asking it to write for you. Do this once and every piece of content it

produces will feel like you wrote it. Voice training prompt — run this first: I'm going to give you 3 examples of captions I've written. Study the tone, word choice, sentence

length, and personality. Then write all future social media content in this style. Example 1: [paste a caption you've written] Example 2: [paste another caption] Example 3: [paste another caption] Confirm you understand my voice.

Rebooking — The Highest-ROI Communication You Can Send The math: If your average service is $120 and you rebook just 2 extra clients per week, that's $240/week — $12,480 per year — from messages that take seconds to generate and a few minutes to send.

Rebooking reminder text (send 4 weeks after appointment): Write a short, friendly text message from a hair stylist to a client who is due for a [SERVICE] appointment. The message should: feel personal (not automated), mention approximately when they

were last in, suggest a timeframe to come back, and include a simple way to book. Keep it under 60 words. Rebooking reminder text (client overdue by 6+ weeks): Write a casual, non-pushy text to a hair salon client who is 6 weeks overdue for their [SERVICE]

appointment. Tone should be warm and a little playful — like a text from a friend, not a business. Mention what tends to happen when you wait too long, and make it easy to reply with a day that works. Review Management

Responding to reviews — both positive and negative — is one of the most valuable things you can do for your Google ranking and your reputation. AI makes it fast. Respond to a 5-star review:

Write a response to this 5-star Google review for my salon: [PASTE REVIEW]. The response should: thank them by name, reference something specific from their review, reflect my salon's personality, and invite them back. Under 80 words.

Respond to a negative review (without being defensive): Write a professional, calm response to this negative Google review: [PASTE REVIEW]. The response should: acknowledge their experience without admitting fault, express genuine concern, invite them

to contact me directly to resolve it, and not make the situation worse. Tone: mature, empathetic, professional. Under 100 words.

Chapter 5

AI for Growth

New client acquisition, Google visibility, referral systems, and getting found online.

Your Google Presence Is Your #1 Growth Lever More new clients find salons through Google Search and Google Maps than through Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook combined. AI helps you optimize every part of your Google Business Profile —

the place most salon owners completely ignore. The Google Business Profile Checklist: Complete every section (services, hours, photos, description, Q&A). Post at least twice per month

using Google Posts. Respond to every review within 48 hours. Add new photos weekly. Answer your own Q&A section with keyword-rich responses. Write your Google Business description:

Write a Google Business Profile description for my hair salon. Salon name: [NAME]. Location: [CITY, STATE]. Services: [LIST TOP SERVICES]. What makes us different: [YOUR DIFFERENTIATOR]. The

description should: be 250 words or less, include natural keywords, communicate our vibe, and end with a call to book. No keyword stuffing — write for humans first. Answer your own Google Q&A:

Create 8 Q&A entries for my Google Business Profile for a hair salon specializing in [SERVICES]. Questions should be what real clients actually search for. Answers should be helpful, include natural keywords, and be under 200 words each.
