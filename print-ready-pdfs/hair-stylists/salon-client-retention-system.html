System 1

The In-Chair Experience System

Retention starts before the appointment ends. How you run your chair determines whether a client

books again — or quietly tries someone else. This system gives you a consistent framework for every appointment. The 5-Part Appointment Framework 1 The Arrival Greeting (first 2 minutes)

• Use their name immediately — 'Hi [name], so great to see you!'

• Reference something personal from last visit or their intake form: 'How did that balayage hold

up in the summer?'

• Offer water, coffee, or tea before they sit down — this is hospitality, not just service

• Never start talking business (rebooking, retail) in the first 2 minutes

2 The Consultation (even for regulars)

• Every appointment gets a brief consultation — even for clients you've seen 50 times

• Then: 'What do you think?' — wait for their reaction before adding anything

• This pause is important: rushing the reveal undercuts the emotional impact of your work

System 2

The Rebooking System The best time to rebook a client is while they're still in your chair, looking at results they love. The second best time is within 48 hours of their appointment. Everything after that gets harder. In-Chair Rebooking Scripts

Use one of these at checkout — before payment, while they're in front of the mirror: For color clients: "To keep this looking its best, I'd love to see you back in about [6-8] weeks. I have [date] and [date] available — which works better for you?"

For cut clients: "Your hair shape really holds well at about [4-6] weeks — after that you start to lose the line. I can get you on the books right now while I have good availability. What works for you?" For treatment clients:

"For the best results from this treatment, I recommend coming back in [4] weeks for a maintenance pass. Want to lock that in while you're here?" The Key Principle: Give Two Options, Not an Open-Ended Question

'Would you like to rebook?' gives them a yes/no. 'I have Tuesday the 12th or Thursday the 14th — which works better?' gives them a choice between two yeses. Use the second format every time. Post-Appointment Rebooking Texts

If they didn't rebook in the chair, send this text 3–4 weeks after their appointment: 4-week reminder (color client): Hey [name]! It's been about a month since your last color — just wanted to check in and see how

it's holding up. I have some openings coming up if you're thinking about coming in. Just reply and I'll send you options! 6-week overdue (any service): Hey [name]! Miss seeing you in the chair It's been a little while — [service] tends to start showing

wear around now. I've got a couple spots open this week and next if you want to get back in. Just say the word! 8+ weeks overdue: Hey [name]! It's been a minute — hope everything's good with you! I know life gets busy. Whenever

you're ready to come back in, just reach out and I'll get you sorted. I always have time for you.

System 3

The Follow-Up System One touchpoint after an appointment can dramatically increase the chance a client returns. Most stylists skip this entirely — which means you can stand out with minimal effort. The 24-Hour Follow-Up Text

Send this the day after every appointment. Takes 30 seconds. Works every time: After color: Hey [name]! Just checking in — how are you loving your hair today? Hope you're getting lots of

compliments Remember: wait 48 hours before washing, and use sulfate-free shampoo to keep that color vibrant as long as possible! After a big change (chop, color transformation, etc.):

Hey [name]! Still thinking about your transformation yesterday — you looked absolutely amazing. How are you feeling about it today? First-day nerves are normal after a big change, but I have a feeling you're going to love it more every day

After a regular maintenance appointment: Hey [name]! Thanks so much for coming in yesterday — always love seeing you! Let me know if you have any questions about the products I recommended. See you in [X] weeks!

The Review Ask (send 48 hours post-appointment) The best time to ask for a review is when the client is still in the glow of their new look — 24–48 hours after their appointment:

Hey [name]! So glad you came in — you looked incredible. If you have 2 minutes, I'd love it if you left me a Google review. It genuinely means the world to small businesses like mine and helps other

The Win-Back System A client who hasn't booked in 90+ days is at serious risk of not coming back. But most of them haven't left on purpose — they just got busy, lost the habit, or assumed you were too booked. A

well-timed win-back message brings a significant percentage back. The Win-Back Sequence (3 messages over 10 days) Message 1 — Day 1 (Warm Reconnect)

Subject: We miss you at [Salon Name]

Hey [name]! It's been a little while and I wanted to reach out personally. I always loved seeing you in my chair and I hope everything's been great. I've had a few openings come up and thought of you —

no pressure at all, but if you've been thinking about coming in, I'd love to get you back on the books.

Identifying Your VIP Clients A VIP client meets at least 2 of these criteria:

• Books 4+ times per year

• Average ticket is above your menu average

• Has referred at least one friend in the past year

• Has left a Google review

• Has been coming to you for 2+ years

The 3 Referral Program Structures Structure How It Works Best For Give $20, Get $20 Referring client gets $20 off next visit when their friend books. New client gets $20 off first visit. Easy to explain and track. Works for any salon size.

Service Upgrade Reward Referring client gets a complimentary add-on (gloss, scalp treatment, blowout) on their next visit. Protects margins better than discounts. Feels more premium. Points-Based Loyalty

Clients earn points for visits, referrals, and reviews. Redeem for free services or products. Best for salons with a booking system that supports tracking.

Referral Ask Script (send 1–2 days after a great appointment):

Hey [name]! I'm so glad you loved your [service] — you looked incredible. If you have any friends who've been looking for a new stylist, I'd absolutely love the referral. Anyone you send my way gets

[offer], and I'll take care of you too with [reward]. Thanks for being such an amazing client VIP Recognition Touchpoints These small gestures have outsized impact on client loyalty: Birthday message

Send a birthday text or email with a small offer (complimentary add-on, priority booking). Use

Prompt #19 from the Prompt Library.

Anniversary acknowledgment When a client hits 1 year or a milestone visit (10th appointment, 5-year anniversary), acknowledge it in chair and on social (with permission).
