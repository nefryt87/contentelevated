Build a practice brand that earns trust before the first appointment. Color palettes • Fonts • Canva templates • 7-day launch checklist

SECTION 1: PRACTICE POSITIONING

Patients choose a chiropractor based on trust, proximity, and reputation — in that order. Your brand is what builds trust before they ever call. A clear, consistent, professional

visual identity signals that you take your practice as seriously as you take patient care. Your Practice Promise We help [patient type — e.g., active adults / families / desk workers / athletes]

[achieve result — e.g., get out of pain and stay that way / move better and feel stronger / recover from injury without surgery] through [your approach — e.g., gentle, evidence-based chiropractic care / personalized treatment plans / whole- body wellness].

Example: 'We help active families in [City] get out of pain, move better, and stay healthy — without relying on medication or surgery.' Practice Personality (Pick 2–3)

• Trustworthy — You do what you say and say what you mean

• Caring — Every patient is a person, not a case number

• Expert — You explain conditions clearly and set realistic expectations

• Local — You're part of this community, not a franchise

• Proactive — You reach out before problems escalate

SECTION 3: TYPOGRAPHY

USE FONT NOTES

Headlines Merriweather Bold Authoritative and trustworthy — signals expertise. Free on Google Fonts. Subheadings Nunito SemiBold Friendly yet professional — approachable without being casual.

Body Copy Open Sans Regular Clean, highly readable on all screens and print sizes. CTA / Callouts Nunito Bold For buttons, stat callouts, and key messages. Use sparingly.

SECTION 5: PHOTO & VIDEO GUIDE

Practice Photography Tips

• Clinic photos: Clean, bright, uncluttered — patients are evaluating whether they'll

feel comfortable

• Team photos: Approachable smiles, professional but not stiff — white coat

optional depending on brand

• Action shots: Treatment in progress (with patient consent) convert well — shows

patients what to expect

• Lighting: Natural light from windows or a ring light — avoid harsh overhead

fluorescents
