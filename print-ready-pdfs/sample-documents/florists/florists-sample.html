Prompt System

Start with the client moment.

Use these prompts when the customer is asking, deciding, rebooking, reviewing, or needing a reason to say yes.

01

Copy / paste prompt

Wedding Floral Inquiry Response

Write a warm, enthusiastic inquiry response email for my floral studio. The bride [Name] reached out about wedding florals for her wedding on [date] at [venue]. She mentioned: [paste details — style, flowers she loves, mood board description]. My studio aesthetic: [your style]. I want to: (1) make her feel I genuinely understand her vision, (2) briefly describe how I would interpret her style, (3) mention what a consultation looks like, (4) invite her to book a call. Warm and evocative. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

02

Copy / paste prompt

Post-Consultation Proposal Follow-Up

Write a post-consultation proposal follow-up email for my floral studio. Client [Name], consultation was [date]. Proposing florals for [date] wedding at [venue], approximate investment $[amount]. I want to: (1) recap the vision we discussed in 2-3 evocative sentences, (2) briefly summarise the key arrangements, (3) present the investment and next step confidently. Evocative and warm. Under 250 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

03

Copy / paste prompt

Corporate Flower Subscription Proposal

Write a corporate flower subscription proposal for my floral studio. Client: [Company] at [office/hotel/restaurant]. Proposing weekly arrangements for [locations]. Investment: $[amount] per week covering [what is included]. I want to: (1) describe the impact of beautiful flowers on a professional environment, (2) present the service clearly, (3) offer a trial arrangement. Professional and compelling. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

04

Copy / paste prompt

Wedding Planner Introduction Email

Write a professional introduction email from a florist to a wedding planner named [Name] at [Company]. I want to: (1) introduce my studio and aesthetic briefly, (2) explain how my approach as a florist makes a planner's job easier, (3) propose a coffee or call to see if there is a mutual fit. Warm and professional. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

05

Copy / paste prompt

Instagram Caption Batch (5 Captions)

Write 5 Instagram captions for a florist specialising in [your style]. Topics: (1) a recent wedding or event reveal — the flowers, the venue, the atmosphere (anonymised), (2) a seasonal flower you are obsessed with right now and why, (3) what goes into designing wedding florals that clients never see, (4) a common misconception about wedding floristry you want to address, (5) a direct inquiry CTA for the upcoming season. Each under 150 words, evocative and specific. Studio name: [Studio Name].
