Prompt System

Start with the client moment.

Use these prompts when the customer is asking, deciding, rebooking, reviewing, or needing a reason to say yes.

01

Copy / paste prompt

Same-Day Inquiry Response Email

Write a warm, enthusiastic inquiry response email for my personal styling practice. The potential client [Name] reached out about [service type — e.g., wardrobe audit / corporate styling / total style transformation]. They mentioned: [paste key details from their inquiry]. My styling philosophy is [your approach]. I want to: (1) make them feel genuinely understood, (2) briefly describe how I'd approach their specific situation, (3) invite them to a discovery call with a specific time offered. Warm, confident, under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].

02

Copy / paste prompt

Post-Discovery Call Follow-Up

Write a post-discovery call follow-up email for my personal styling practice. I just spoke with [Name] about [their goal/situation]. Key things they shared: [list 3-4 things — wardrobe frustration, body confidence, lifestyle needs, upcoming life change]. I'm proposing [service/package] at $[amount]. I want this email to: (1) mirror back what they told me they most need in their own words, (2) briefly explain how my approach addresses it, (3) present the next step clearly. Warm and confident, under 250 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].

03

Copy / paste prompt

Instagram Caption Batch (5 Captions)

Write 5 Instagram captions for a personal stylist who specialises in [niche — e.g., corporate women / plus-size styling / bridal party]. Topics: (1) the most common wardrobe mistake clients make before working with you, (2) a capsule wardrobe formula for their specific lifestyle, (3) how to dress for a specific situation relevant to your niche, (4) what personal styling costs vs. what clients assume, (5) a client transformation story (anonymised). Each under 150 words, warm and specific, ending with a question or soft CTA. Business name: [Business Name].

04

Copy / paste prompt

Photographer Partnership Introduction

Write a professional introduction email from a personal stylist to a photographer named [Name] who specialises in [headshots / branding / weddings]. I want to propose a mutual referral relationship — my clients need photography, their clients benefit from styling guidance before shoots. I want to: (1) introduce myself briefly, (2) explain how styling before a shoot improves results for both of us, (3) propose a collaboration or mutual referral arrangement. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].

05

Copy / paste prompt

Post-Service Review Request

Write a post-service review request email for my personal styling practice. Client [Name] just completed [wardrobe audit / shopping session / total transformation package]. I want to: (1) celebrate what they achieved with one specific personal observation, (2) make a warm personal ask for a Google review, (3) include the direct link. Emotional and genuine — not automated-feeling. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].
