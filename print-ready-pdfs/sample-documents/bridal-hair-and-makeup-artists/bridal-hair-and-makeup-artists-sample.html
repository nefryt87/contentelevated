Use these prompts when the customer is asking, deciding, rebooking, reviewing, or needing a reason to say yes.

01

Copy / paste prompt

Same-Day Bridal Inquiry Response

Write a warm, enthusiastic bridal inquiry response email for my hair and makeup studio. The bride [Name] reached out about her wedding on [date] at [venue]. She mentioned: [paste details from her inquiry — style, party size, concerns]. My speciality is [your speciality — e.g., romantic bridal / natural glow / bold editorial bridal]. I want to: (1) make her feel like I genuinely read her inquiry, (2) briefly describe how I'd approach her specific wedding, (3) mention what's included in my bridal packages, (4) invite her to a trial or consultation. Warm, excited, under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

02

Copy / paste prompt

Trial Run Booking Confirmation

Write a trial run booking confirmation email for my bridal hair and makeup studio. Bride [Name], trial on [date] at [time] at [location]. I want to cover: (1) what to bring and how to prepare — clean hair, clean moisturised skin, inspo photos, accessories to test, (2) what to expect during the session, (3) that we'll document the approved look at the end, (4) how the trial connects to wedding day. Practical and excited, under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

03

Copy / paste prompt

Wedding Morning Timeline Email

Write a wedding morning getting-ready timeline email for my bridal hair and makeup studio. Bride [Name], wedding [date]. Party: [breakdown — e.g., 1 bride, 4 bridesmaids, 2 mothers]. I need [X] minutes per person for hair, [X] minutes for makeup. Ceremony starts at [time], bride needs to be ready by [time]. I want to: (1) present the timeline clearly showing each person in sequence, (2) explain what the bride needs ready — lighting, space, mirrors, (3) confirm my arrival time. Clear and professional. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

Wedding Photographer Introduction

Write a professional introduction email from a bridal hair and makeup artist to a wedding photographer named [Name]. I want to: (1) introduce myself and my bridal speciality briefly, (2) mention that well-prepared hair and makeup dramatically affects photo results, (3) propose a mutual referral relationship or a styled shoot collaboration. Warm and professional, under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

05

Copy / paste prompt

Instagram Caption Batch (5 Captions)

Write 5 Instagram captions for a bridal hair and makeup artist. Topics: (1) a wedding morning story — emotional, specific, one moment that stood out (change names), (2) how to prepare for a bridal trial — practical tips that help brides and demonstrate expertise, (3) a bridal trend you're loving right now and why it photographs beautifully, (4) what to look for when choosing your bridal hair and makeup artist, (5) a direct booking CTA for current season. Each under 150 words, warm and specific, ending with a question or soft CTA. Studio name: [Studio Name].
