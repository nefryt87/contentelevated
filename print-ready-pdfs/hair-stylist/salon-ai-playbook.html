Why AI Is the Unfair Advantage Every Salon

Owner Needs Right Now

Let's be honest. Running a salon is one of the hardest small businesses there is. You're the owner,

the stylist, the marketer, the HR department, and sometimes the janitor — all in the same day.

You're behind the chair for eight hours and then expected to show up on social media, answer DMs,

send rebooking reminders, write job posts, respond to reviews, and somehow still have energy left

over.

Most marketing advice isn't built for you. It assumes you have a team, a budget, and hours of free

time. You have none of those things.

AI changes that equation. Not because it replaces you — it absolutely cannot do what you do

behind the chair. But it can handle the thinking, writing, and planning that eats your time and

mental energy outside of it. This playbook shows you exactly how.

Before you start prompting, give ChatGPT context about your business once. This is called a system

prompt or custom instruction — it means every response will automatically be tailored to your

salon without you having to explain it every time.

is.

Your First Win: Write This Week's Instagram Caption

Open ChatGPT and try this prompt right now. It will take about 30 seconds and produce something

better than most people write in 20 minutes.

Write 3 Instagram captions for my salon. One should promote [SERVICE], one should be a

behind-the-scenes post about [SOMETHING FROM THIS WEEK], and one should be an engagement

question for my followers. Keep each caption under 150 words, use a conversational tone, and end

each with a call to action.

The Golden Rule of Prompting:

The more specific you are, the better the output. Vague prompt = generic response. Specific

prompt = something you can actually use. Always include: what you want, who it's for, the tone,

and any details that make your salon unique.

Essential Marketing Prompts

Promotional post without sounding salesy:

discount — think priority booking or a free add-on), and end with a direct link CTA. Tone: friendly,

personal, like a note from the stylist.

Rebooking — The Highest-ROI Communication You Can Send

The math: If your average service is $120 and you rebook just 2 extra clients per week, that's

$240/week — $12,480 per year — from messages that take seconds to generate and a few minutes

to send.

Rebooking reminder text (send 4 weeks after appointment):

Write a short, friendly text message from a hair stylist to a client who is due for a [SERVICE]

appointment. The message should: feel personal (not automated), mention approximately when they

were last in, suggest a timeframe to come back, and include a simple way to book. Keep it under 60

words.

Rebooking reminder text (client overdue by 6+ weeks):

Write a casual, non-pushy text to a hair salon client who is 6 weeks overdue for their [SERVICE]

appointment. Tone should be warm and a little playful — like a text from a friend, not a business.

Mention what tends to happen when you wait too long, and make it easy to reply with a day that

works.

Review Management

Responding to reviews — both positive and negative — is one of the most valuable things you can

Respond to a 5-star review:

thank them by name, reference something specific from their review, reflect my salon's personality,

and invite them back. Under 80 words.

Consultation Scripts

A strong consultation converts uncertain clients into confident ones — and confident clients spend

more, rebook faster, and refer more friends.

Build a consultation script for any service:

Create a consultation script for a hair stylist about to do a [SERVICE] on a new client. Include: 5

questions to understand their hair history and goals, 2 questions to uncover lifestyle and

maintenance habits, key things to look for during the hair assessment, and language to set realistic

expectations if needed. Format as a natural conversation guide.

Script for raising your prices:

Write a short, confident script for a hair stylist to tell a long-term client that their prices are

increasing by [AMOUNT] starting [DATE]. The message should: be direct and unapologetic,

acknowledge the relationship, briefly justify the increase, and thank them for their loyalty. Format:

short text or email version.

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

SOPs are the difference between a salon that runs smoothly when you're not there and one that

falls apart. AI can draft a full SOP in minutes — you just need to review it and add your specific

details.

Create an SOP for any process:

Write a standard operating procedure for [PROCESS: e.g., opening the salon, client check-in, color

mixing, closing procedures, retail upselling]. Format it as a numbered step-by-step checklist.

Include: who is responsible, what tools or supplies are needed, any quality checkpoints, and what to

do if something goes wrong.

New staff onboarding guide:

Create a first-week onboarding guide for a new stylist joining my salon. Include: day-by-day

breakdown of their first 5 days, what they need to learn by when, who to go to with different

questions, key policies to understand immediately, and what success looks like at the end of week

one.

Pricing Strategy

Use AI as a thinking partner when you're working through pricing decisions. It can't access your

Think through a price increase:

Help me think through raising my prices. My current prices: [LIST KEY SERVICES AND PRICES]. My

Your Google Presence Is Your #1 Growth Lever

More new clients find salons through Google Search and Google Maps than through Instagram,

TikTok, and Facebook combined. AI helps you optimize every part of your Google Business Profile —

the place most salon owners completely ignore.

The Google Business Profile Checklist:

Complete every section (services, hours, photos, description, Q&A). Post at least twice per month

using Google Posts. Respond to every review within 48 hours. Add new photos weekly. Answer your

own Q&A section with keyword-rich responses.

Write your Google Business description:

Write a Google Business Profile description for my hair salon. Salon name: [NAME]. Location: [CITY,

STATE]. Services: [LIST TOP SERVICES]. What makes us different: [YOUR DIFFERENTIATOR]. The

description should: be 250 words or less, include natural keywords, communicate our vibe, and end

with a call to book. No keyword stuffing — write for humans first.

Answer your own Google Q&A:

Create 8 Q&A entries for my Google Business Profile for a hair salon specializing in [SERVICES].

Questions should be what real clients actually search for. Answers should be helpful, include natural

keywords, and be under 200 words each.

When AI Output Isn't Quite Right

AI rarely nails it perfectly on the first try — especially for tone and voice. Here are the refinement

prompts that fix 90% of issues.

Fix: Too formal / corporate

Rewrite this in a more casual, conversational tone. Like how I'd actually talk to a client, not a press

release.

Fix: Too long

Shorten this to under [X] words without losing the key points.

Fix: Doesn't sound like me

Rewrite this using the voice examples I gave you earlier. It should sound more like [describe your

style].

Fix: Missing a call to action

Add a natural call to action at the end that asks them to [book / DM / call / reply].

Fix: Too salesy

Rewrite this to feel less like an ad. Lead with value or information, and make the offer feel like a

helpful suggestion.
