SECTION 1: ANNUAL POLICY REVIEW SCRIPTS

Annual Review Call Opening

SCRIPT — ANNUAL POLICY REVIEW CALL

YOU: Hi [First Name], thanks for taking the time — this won't take long and I promise it's worth it. YOU: My main goal today is to make sure your coverage still makes sense for where you are in life. Things change — income, family situation, assets — and your

insurance should change with them. YOU: Can I ask a few quick questions to make sure nothing's shifted? [Ask: any new family members / life events, income changes, new property, health changes, any claims filed]

[Listen more than you talk on review calls. The best adjustments come from what the client volunteers, not from a product pitch.] YOU: Based on what you've shared, here's what I'd like to revisit: [specific coverage gap or update identified].

YOU: Would you like me to run the numbers on the adjustment and send you a comparison? Shouldn't take more than [timeframe].

SECTION 2: OBJECTION HANDLING SCRIPTS

Objection: Already Have Work Coverage

OBJECTION — ALREADY HAVE WORK COVERAGE

YOU: That's great — group coverage is a solid foundation. My only concern is that group policies have a couple of limitations worth knowing about. YOU: The main ones: the coverage amount is often fixed and may not be enough

based on your actual income and obligations. And if you ever leave your job — voluntarily or not — you lose the coverage, often right when you might need it most. YOU: I'm not suggesting replacing it — I'm suggesting we look at whether it's actually

sufficient and if not, what a small individual policy might look like to fill the gap. YOU: Want me to run a quick comparison? Takes about 15 minutes. Objection: Can't Afford It Right Now

OBJECTION — CAN'T AFFORD IT

YOU: I hear you — and I want to respect that. Can I ask one question: what would happen financially to your family / business / assets if something happened to you tomorrow and you didn't have coverage? [Let them answer. Don't jump in.]

YOU: The reason I ask is that can't afford insurance and can't afford to be uninsured often mean very different things when you actually run the numbers. YOU: Let me put together the most cost-effective option for your situation —

sometimes people are genuinely surprised how manageable the monthly cost is for meaningful coverage. Can I do that and send it over? Objection: Need to Talk to My Spouse

OBJECTION — NEED TO TALK TO SPOUSE

YOU: Of course — this absolutely should be a joint decision. Would it be helpful if I put together a one-page summary you could share with them before you discuss it? YOU: Or even better — would your spouse have 15 minutes to join a quick call so I

can answer both your questions at once? Joint calls are usually much more efficient. [Always offer a concrete next step. 'Talk to your spouse' without a next step usually means the conversation dies.]
