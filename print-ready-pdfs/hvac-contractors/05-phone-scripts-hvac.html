SECTION 1: INBOUND CALL SCRIPTS

Standard Service Call

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — STANDARD SERVICE CALL

DISPATCHER: Thank you for calling [Company Name], this is [Name] — how can I

help you today?

[Listen fully. Let them describe the problem without

interrupting.]

DISPATCHER: Got it — that's something we can definitely take care of. Let me get a

couple of quick details.

[Collect: full name, address, best callback number, problem

description, urgency level]

DISPATCHER: We have availability [today/tomorrow]. Our techs are licensed and

insured — they'll call 30 minutes before arrival. Does [time window] work?

[Confirm. Read back the address. Always.]

DISPATCHER: Perfect — you're all set. Anything else before I let you go?

[KEY: A calm professional dispatcher builds trust before the tech

ever arrives.]

Price-Shopping Call — How Much Does It Cost?

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — PRICE SHOPPING

CUSTOMER: How much do you charge for [problem]?

DISPATCHER: Great question — I want to give you an honest number, not one I'll

have to walk back. The cost depends on what's actually causing the issue.

DISPATCHER: Our diagnostic fee is $[amount] and applies toward the repair. For

standard tune-ups, customers typically pay $[X]. Larger issues depend on what we

find.

DISPATCHER: Can I get you scheduled? Our tech gives you an exact price before

any work starts — zero surprises.

[Never give firm prices for diagnostic work. Ranges are fine for

standard services.]

SECTION 3: OBJECTION HANDLING

Objection: It's Too Expensive

OBJECTION — TOO EXPENSIVE

TECH: I hear you — more than you were hoping for. Can I ask what budget you were

working with? I want to see if there's a way to make this work.

TECH: We offer financing — you could spread this over [X] months at low or zero

interest. Works out to about $[monthly amount] a month.

TECH: I also want to be honest: if you're comparing to a lower quote, it's worth

asking what's included — licensing, insurance, warranty. We're licensed, insured,

with a [X]-year workmanship guarantee.

[Never just repeat the price. Address the real objection —

budget, value, or trust.]

Objection: Want to Think About It

OBJECTION — WANT TO THINK ABOUT IT

DISPATCHER: Absolutely — take the time you need. Can I ask: is there anything

specific you're weighing? Sometimes I can answer it right now.

DISPATCHER: If it helps, we're booking [timeframe] out for [service type]. I can hold a

slot for [24/48 hours] while you decide.

[Give a soft reason to decide sooner. Never pressure, never

guilt.]
