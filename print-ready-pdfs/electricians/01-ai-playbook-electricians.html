ELECTRICIAN

The AI Playbook for Electrical Contractors Convert more estimates, sell service agreements, win commercial accounts, and build the Google reputation that makes your phone ring first

INTRODUCTION

The electrical contractors dominating their local markets aren't just the best technicians. They follow up on estimates within 48 hours. They win commercial accounts with a professional proposal that most competitors never send. They sell whole-home

electrical inspections that generate 3-5 additional jobs per customer per year. And they have a Google reputation that does their selling before they pick up the phone. AI makes all of it systematic. This playbook gives you 7 chapters covering every high-

leverage scenario — with copy-paste prompts throughout.

CHAPTER 1: ESTIMATE FOLLOW-UP &

CONVERSION

The 48-Hour Follow-Up Window Electrical estimates that aren't followed up within 48 hours convert at a fraction of the rate of those that are. Most homeowners are getting 2-3 quotes — the contractor who

follows up professionally and specifically wins a disproportionate number of jobs.

PROMPT — ESTIMATE FOLLOW-UP EMAIL

Write a professional estimate follow-up email for my electrical contracting business. The customer [Name] received an estimate for [job type] on [date] for $[amount]. I want to: (1) confirm they received it, (2) briefly explain what's included that a lower

bid might not cover (licensed, insured, permit pulled if required, [X]-year workmanship guarantee), (3) make the next step obvious. Professional and warm, not pushy. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Company Name]. The 3-Touch Follow-Up Sequence

CHAPTER 5: SOCIAL CONTENT & BRAND

Content That Builds Trust With Homeowners

Homeowners hire electricians they trust — and social media builds trust before a single conversation happens. The content pillars that convert best for electrical contractors: education (safety, what to look for), social proof (completed jobs, reviews), and

seasonal awareness (when to call).

PROMPT — FACEBOOK & GOOGLE POST BATCH

Write a post-job review request text message and email for my electrical contracting business. Customer [Name] just had [job type] completed at their home. The text version should be under 60 words. The email version should be under 100 words.

Both should: make a personal, non-automated-feeling ask, include the direct Google review link, and express genuine appreciation. Sign as [Your Name], [Company Name]. Responding to Reviews
