Phone & Voicemail Script Playbook

Inbound call scripts, voicemails that get returned, upsell conversations, and review responses

SECTION 1: INBOUND CALL SCRIPTS

Standard Service Call

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — STANDARD INBOUND

DISPATCHER: [Company Name], this is [Name] — what's going on for you today? [Listen fully. Don't interrupt. Let them describe the problem completely.] DISPATCHER: Got it — that's something we can take care of. Let me get a few details

so I can send the right person. [Collect: name, address, callback number, problem, urgency — running water? wet area?] DISPATCHER: We have [time available]. Our plumbers are licensed and insured — they'll call 30 minutes before arrival. Does that work?

[Confirm everything. Read back the address.] Emergency Burst Pipe / Active Leak

DISPATCHER SCRIPT — EMERGENCY LEAK

DISPATCHER: [Company Name] — sounds like an emergency. Where are you located? [Get address immediately. Water damage is time-sensitive. Every minute matters.] DISPATCHER: First thing — if you can safely reach your main water shutoff, turn it

off now. It's usually near the water meter, under a sink, or in the basement. Do you know where yours is? [Guide them through shutoff while dispatching the nearest tech simultaneously]

DISPATCHER: I'm reaching out to our nearest tech right now. We'll have someone heading your way within [timeframe]. [Call back within 5 minutes with confirmed ETA. Helping the customer stop the water before the tech arrives is a genuine differentiator.]

Price-Shopping Call

SECTION 2: VOICEMAIL SCRIPTS

Estimate Follow-Up

VOICEMAIL — ESTIMATE FOLLOW-UP

Hi [First Name], it's [Your Name] from [Company Name] — just following up on the estimate for [job type] from [day]. Wanted to make sure you got it and see if you had any questions.

We're available [timeframe] if you want to move forward. Call me at [Phone] — [repeat number]. No pressure at all. [Under 25 seconds. Name, company, reason, number twice.] Seasonal Winterization

VOICEMAIL — SEASONAL WINTERIZATION

Hi [First Name], [Your Name] from [Company Name]. Winter is coming up fast and we're

booking winterization checks for past customers first. A winter check is $[amount], takes

about an hour, and can prevent a frozen pipe situation that costs thousands to fix. Call me at [Phone] — [repeat number]. Thanks! Water Heater Age Alert

VOICEMAIL — WATER HEATER AGE ALERT

Hi [First Name], [Your Name] from [Company Name]. Calling because your water heater is now around [X] years old — at that age it's worth a quick look, not to sell you anything, just

to know where you stand. Proactive replacement is a lot less disruptive than an emergency one. Call me at [Phone] — [repeat number].

TECH SCRIPT — WATER HEATER REPLACEMENT

TECH: I want to be straight with you about this water heater. It's [X] years old and this is the second time we've been out here for it. TECH: I can repair it — about $[amount] — but honestly at this age you're probably

looking at another call within [timeframe]. A new unit is $[amount] installed with a [X]- year warranty. The math usually favors replacement past [X] years. TECH: But it's your call — I'll do whichever you decide. I just want you to have the full picture.

[Always present both options honestly. Customers who feel informed don't have regret — and they refer.] Soft Add-On During Service

TECH SCRIPT — SOFT ADD-ON

TECH: I noticed your drain is running a bit slow while I was working nearby. Not an emergency — just something worth knowing. TECH: I can clear that out for $[amount] while I'm already here. Takes 20 minutes and

saves you the service call fee later. Totally up to you. [Only mention genuine observations. Never manufacture problems. Honest add-ons build loyalty.]
