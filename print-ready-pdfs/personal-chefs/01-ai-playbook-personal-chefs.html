PERSONAL CHEF

The AI Playbook for Personal Chefs & Private Chefs Convert more inquiries, present proposals with confidence, build realtor and event planner partnerships, and create a client experience that generates referrals

INTRODUCTION

The personal chefs with full client rosters and premium rates aren't just the most technically skilled in the kitchen. They respond to every inquiry professionally and specifically — making potential clients feel understood before they've ever tasted the

food. They present proposals that communicate value before they present a number. They maintain relationships with realtors, concierge services, and event planners who send warm referrals year-round. They build a personal brand on Instagram and

YouTube that positions them as the expert their ideal client is searching for. And they create a client experience so consistently excellent that every meal becomes a referral. This playbook gives you 7 chapters covering every high-leverage scenario — from

private dining to weekly meal prep to corporate catering — with copy-paste AI prompts throughout.

CHAPTER 1: INQUIRY RESPONSE & PROPOSAL

CONVERSION

The Same-Day Inquiry Response System High-value clients hiring a private chef are making a significant investment and often contact 2-3 chefs simultaneously. A response that feels personalised, professional, and

knowledgeable within a few hours wins a disproportionate number of initial consultations.

CHAPTER 2: PARTNERSHIP & REFERRAL

SYSTEM

Building Referral Partnerships The personal chefs with the most consistently full rosters rarely rely on advertising. They maintain active referral relationships with luxury real estate agents (who sell

homes to exactly the right client), concierge services, event planners, and high-end property managers. One strong realtor relationship can generate 10-20 qualified referrals per year.

PROMPT — LUXURY REALTOR INTRODUCTION

Write a professional introduction email from a personal chef to a luxury real estate agent named [Name] at [Brokerage]. I want to: (1) introduce my private chef service and client profile briefly, (2) mention how my service is an amenity that elevates a

luxury home purchase or listing experience, (3) propose either a referral arrangement or a co-marketing idea — e.g., a private chef dinner for their VIP clients or new homeowners. Professional and specific. Under 150 words. Sign as

[Your Name], [Business Name].

PROMPT — EVENT PLANNER PARTNERSHIP

Write a professional introduction email from a personal chef to a wedding or event planner named [Name] at [Company]. I want to: (1) introduce my chef service and culinary style, (2) explain how I complement their event planning service — intimate

rehearsal dinners, post-wedding brunches, client appreciation dinners, (3) propose a mutual referral relationship. Warm and professional. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].

The Pre-Event Client Experience Clients who feel fully prepared, heard, and cared for before the first knife hits the board arrive relaxed and excited — and relaxed, excited clients become the most enthusiastic

referrers. Pre-event communication is where the client experience is actually made.

PROMPT — PRE-DINNER CONSULTATION CONFIRMATION

Client [Name], consultation call on [date] at [time]. For their event on [event date] —

[brief description]. I want to cover: (1) what we'll discuss on the call — menu direction, dietary restrictions, service preferences, logistics, (2) anything they should prepare or think about beforehand, (3) my enthusiasm for the event. Warm and

preparatory. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].

CHAPTER 4: CONTENT STRATEGY

Instagram, YouTube & Pinterest Strategy for Personal Chefs

Content is the most powerful tool a personal chef has for demonstrating skill and

building trust before a client ever tastes their food. The accounts that generate consistent inquiries post across 4 pillars: behind-the-scenes (prep, plating, markets), education (techniques, ingredients, recipes), social proof (events, client experiences,

testimonials), and personal story.

PROMPT — INSTAGRAM CAPTION BATCH

Write 5 Instagram captions for a personal chef specialising in [your style]. Topics: (1) a recent private dinner — describe the menu, the occasion, what made it special (anonymise the client), (2) a seasonal ingredient you love right now and how you're

using it, (3) what clients say is the biggest benefit of having a personal chef (besides the food), (4) a behind-the-scenes look at sourcing ingredients for a client — farmers market, specialist supplier, etc., (5) a direct inquiry CTA describing who

you'd love to cook for. Each under 150 words, warm and sensory. Business name: [Business Name].

PROMPT — YOUTUBE / BLOG CONTENT IDEAS

Write 5 YouTube video or blog post ideas for a personal chef. Each should include a compelling title and a brief description of what the content covers. Topics: a signature dish tutorial with the story behind it, a day-in-the-life of a private chef (prep

day for a client event), a seasonal menu planning guide, the most common mistakes

CHAPTER 5: PRICING & DIFFICULT

CONVERSATIONS

Presenting Your Rates With Confidence

PROMPT — PRICING ENQUIRY RESPONSE

Write a response to a client who asked 'what do you charge?' for [service type — e.g., private dinner for 8 / weekly meal prep / vacation chef week]. My rates: [describe your pricing structure]. I want to: (1) give them a realistic range or

structure without committing to a final number before I know the scope, (2) briefly explain what drives the cost (guest count, menu complexity, logistics, grocery budget), (3) invite them to a discovery call for an accurate quote. Confident, not

apologetic. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].

PROMPT — BUDGET OBJECTION RESPONSE

Write a professional response to a potential client who said my rate is higher than they expected. My minimum for [service] is $[amount]. I want to: (1) acknowledge their honesty, (2) briefly explain what's reflected in the rate — ingredients, prep time,

service, cleanup, the experience, (3) present any genuine alternatives — a smaller menu, a different service type, or a trial meal. Warm and confident. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].
