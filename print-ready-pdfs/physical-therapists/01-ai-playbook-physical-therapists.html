Chapter 1: Why AI Is the PT Practice's Growth

Tool Physical therapy practices face a unique set of business challenges: high no-show rates, patients who discharge early before completing their plan of care, physician

referral relationships that require consistent maintenance, and a need to stay visible in

an increasingly competitive local market. None of these are clinical problems. They are communication problems — and AI solves communication problems.

• The No-Show Cost: A single PT no-show costs a practice $75–$200 in lost

revenue. With an average of 3–5 per week, that's $15,000–$50,000 per year in preventable losses. A consistent reminder sequence cuts this significantly.

• The Early Discharge Problem: Studies show that patients who discharge

before completing their plan of care have significantly worse outcomes — and are far less likely to refer. Communication between visits keeps patients engaged and committed to the full plan.

Chapter 2: Your First 3 AI Wins — This Week

Start with one of these. Each takes under an hour to implement and produces immediate, measurable results.

• Win #1 — Appointment Reminder Sequence: A 3-message reminder system

— 48 hours before, morning of, and post no-show recovery — reduces cancellations and fills gaps faster. AI writes all three in under 5 minutes.

• Win #2 — HEP Companion Email: Every home exercise program should be

sent with an AI-written companion email that explains each exercise, addresses the most common mistakes, and encourages the patient. HEP adherence improves. Outcomes improve.

• Win #3 — Physician Thank-You and Update: After treating a referred patient,

AI writes a professional update letter to the referring physician — clinical progress, current status, and a thank-you. Most PT practices never send these consistently. The ones that do get significantly more referrals.

Chapter 3: Reducing No-Shows and Protecting

Your Schedule PT practices that implement a structured reminder system typically see no-show rates drop from 15–20% to under 8%. The barrier isn't wanting to fix this — it's having the

time and system to do it consistently for every patient. AI removes that barrier.

• The 3-Touch Reminder System: 48-hour reminder (confirm or reschedule),

morning-of reminder (logistics and encouragement), no-show recovery message (same day, warm and non-judgmental). Three messages that protect your schedule.

Chapter 4: Patient Retention — Completing

Plans of Care The average PT patient completes only 60–70% of their prescribed plan of care. The gap between prescribed and completed visits represents both revenue loss and suboptimal patient outcomes. Communication is the most powerful lever for improving

both.

• The Between-Visit Check-In: A short message 2–3 days after each visit asking

about soreness, pain levels, and HEP adherence. Takes 90 seconds with AI. Patients feel supported between appointments. Drop-off rates fall significantly.

• The Plateau Acknowledgment: When a patient's progress slows, most stop

coming without saying anything. AI writes a proactive midpoint message that normalizes the plateau, explains the process, and reinforces why continuing matters.

• The Discharge Planning Email: As a patient approaches the end of their plan of

care, AI writes a transition message that recaps their progress, sets self- management expectations, and introduces the option of wellness or maintenance visits.

• The Missed Visit Recovery: When a patient misses a scheduled visit, AI writes

a same-day check-in that acknowledges the missed visit, asks if everything is okay, and makes rebooking frictionless.

Communication That Actually Works HEP adherence is one of the most significant predictors of PT outcomes — and one of the most commonly poor. The difference between patients who do their exercises and

those who don't is almost always communication and understanding. AI makes HEP communication systematic and effortless.

• The HEP Companion Email: Every time a HEP is issued or updated, AI writes a

companion email that explains each exercise in plain language, describes the common mistakes to avoid, explains why each exercise matters for their specific condition, and provides encouragement.

• The HEP Check-In Message: 3–4 days after issuing a new HEP, AI writes a

brief check-in asking if the patient has been able to complete the exercises, whether anything is unclear, and if they've experienced any discomfort.

• The Progress Reinforcement Email: When a patient demonstrates consistent

HEP adherence, AI writes a reinforcement message — acknowledging their commitment and connecting their effort to the improvements you're both observing.

• The HEP Update Communication: When a HEP is progressed or modified, AI

writes a clear update email that explains what changed, why it changed, and what the patient should stop doing — reducing the confusion that causes HEP drop-off.

Chapter 6: Building Your Physician Referral

Network The PT practices with the fullest schedules have the strongest physician relationships. But most PTs never systematically build and maintain these relationships — they rely

on sporadic contact and hope the referrals keep coming. AI makes the outreach and maintenance systematic.

• The Post-Treatment Physician Update: After treating every referred patient, AI

writes a professional progress note update to the referring physician — clinical findings, progress, functional outcomes, and a thank-you. This single habit, done consistently, is the most powerful referral-building tool in PT.

Chapter 7: Social Content That Educates and

Attracts Patients Patients research physical therapists online before they call — and before their physician refers them. PT practices with consistent, educational social content get more

direct-access patients, earn more trust from referring physicians, and convert better. AI makes that consistency achievable alongside a full patient load.

• The 3-Post Formula: One educational post (condition explainer, exercise tip,

myth-bust), one trust post (patient story with consent, team spotlight, behind the scenes), one engagement post (question, poll, seasonal tip). Mon/Wed/Fri.

• High-Converting Topics: Low back pain myths, rotator cuff recovery, ACL

rehab timeline, why 'rest' isn't always the answer, the difference between PT and a personal trainer, dry needling explained, pelvic floor PT, balance and fall prevention for seniors.

• Google Business Profile: For PT practices, Google is the most important

platform for local patient acquisition. Weekly GBP posts, consistent photo updates, and responding to every review drives meaningful new patient volume.
