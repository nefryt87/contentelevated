How to Find the Keywords Buyers Are Actually Using

✓ METHOD 1 — ETSY SEARCH AUTOCOMPLETE (free, most powerful):

✓ Go to Etsy.com. Type your product category in the search bar. DO NOT press

Enter. Note every autocomplete suggestion — these are real buyer searches. Start broad ('silver necklace') then narrow ('personalised silver necklace for women'). Do this for 10-15 starting phrases for each product.

✓

✓ METHOD 2 — ETSY'S OWN SEARCH RESULTS:

✓ Search for your product on Etsy. Scroll to the bottom of the first page — Etsy often

shows 'Related searches' which are real buyer queries. Pin these — they're gold.

✓

✓ METHOD 3 — COMPETITOR LISTINGS:

✓ Find your 3-5 strongest competitors (shops with similar products and many

reviews). Read their titles and descriptions carefully. Note the keywords and phrases they repeat — these are proven search terms.

✓

✓ METHOD 4 — PINTEREST SEARCH:

✓ Pinterest buyers and Etsy buyers overlap significantly. Search your product type on

Pinterest and note the autocomplete suggestions and recommended search filters — these reveal how buyers think about and describe your product type.

✓

✓ METHOD 5 — ERANK OR MARMALEAD (paid tools):

✓ eRank (free tier available) and Marmalead are Etsy-specific SEO tools that show

search volume and competition for specific keywords. Use them to validate which keywords to prioritise.

✓

✓ ORGANISING YOUR KEYWORDS:

✓ Create a simple spreadsheet: column 1 = keyword, column 2 = search volume

estimate (high/medium/low), column 3 = competition (high/medium/low). Prioritise medium competition + high search volume first.
