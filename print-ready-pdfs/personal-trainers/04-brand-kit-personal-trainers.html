BRAND KIT & CANVA TEMPLATE GUIDE

Build a fitness brand that attracts the right clients and charges what you're worth. Color palettes • Fonts • Canva templates • 7-day launch checklist

SECTION 1: BRAND POSITIONING

The most successful personal trainers aren't the most credentialed — they're the most clearly positioned. When someone visits your profile, they should immediately know who you help, what result you deliver, and why you're different. Your Brand Promise

I help [specific person — e.g., busy moms / men over 40 / new runners / postpartum women] [achieve specific result — e.g., lose their first 20 lbs / build functional strength / run their first half marathon] without [biggest fear — e.g.,

spending hours in the gym / giving up the foods they love / getting injured]. Example: 'I help busy moms over 35 get strong and lose the baby weight — without crash diets or 2-hour workouts.'

Brand Personality (Pick 2–3)

• Motivating — You push clients to do what they didn't think was possible

• Real — No filters, no fake transformations, no BS

• Expert — You explain the science simply and clearly

• Supportive — You're in their corner on the hard days

• Results-focused — You care about outcomes, not just effort

Niche Clarity — The Most Important Brand Decision You'll Make Generalist trainers compete on price. Specialist trainers command premium rates. You don't have to turn anyone away — but your marketing should speak to one specific person.

• Postpartum fitness

• Men over 40 / strength and longevity

• Weight loss for busy professionals

• Athletic performance / sport-specific

• Seniors / functional fitness

• Online coaching (any niche, fully remote)
