Section 1: Brand Positioning

The nutrition space is crowded with generic advice. The practitioners who build thriving practices are those who are clearly positioned — who they help, what outcome they deliver, and why their approach is different. Your brand communicates this before a potential client ever speaks to you. Section 5: Content Photo & Video Guide

What to Photograph • Food styling: Real food, natural light, simple props — avoid overly staged or filtered images • Your workspace: Clean desk, notebook, laptop — signals professionalism and approachability • Headshot: Warm, genuine, ideally in a natural or clean clinical setting — not a white coat unless appropriate for your brand • Process shots: Reviewing a meal plan, having a virtual session, cooking demo — shows clients what working with you looks like Short-Form Video Ideas (Instagram Reels / TikTok) • 'Reacting to viral nutrition advice' — one of the highest-reach formats for dietitians • 'What I eat in a day as a dietitian' — high curiosity, strong trust-builder • 'Grocery store walkthrough' — incredibly practical, very shareable • 'Explaining [condition] nutrition in 60 seconds' — establishes niche authority fast • 'Client win story' (with consent) — emotional, high-conversion content
