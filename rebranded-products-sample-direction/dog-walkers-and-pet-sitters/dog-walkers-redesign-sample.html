The Framework

Pet owners are buying peace of mind.

The best dog walkers do more than show availability. They make owners feel safe, informed, and understood before the first leash handoff.

01

Respond fast and warm

Use a first reply that confirms the request, reflects the pet details, and invites a simple next step without sounding scripted.

02

Turn meet-and-greets into bookings

Recap what you learned about the pet, outline how care will work, and make the booking step feel easy.

03

Send care updates that reassure

Short photo check-ins and specific notes help owners feel connected while they are away.

04

Ask for reviews at the right moment

Turn a happy walk, sit, or boarding stay into a natural review or referral request while the experience is fresh.

Prompt System

The first reply should feel calm, capable, and human.

Use the prompt below when a new owner reaches out about recurring walks, pet sitting, boarding, or a trial booking.

New Client Inquiry Response

Write a warm, professional response for my dog walking or pet sitting business. The potential client [Name] reached out about [service]. Their pet is [pet name, breed/species, age] and they mentioned [key details]. Make them feel that their pet will be safe, cared for, and understood. Ask 2-3 helpful questions, briefly explain my approach, and invite them to a meet-and-greet. Keep it under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Business Name].

A

Use specific pet details

Reference the pet’s name, age, routine, behavior notes, or care need so the reply feels personal.

B

Make the next step obvious

Offer one simple action: schedule a meet-and-greet, reply with availability, or book a trial walk.

Implementation

Build the trust loop in one week.

Keep the system simple: one inquiry template, one meet-and-greet follow-up, one update format, one review request, one referral touchpoint.

01

Write your new inquiry reply

Customize the prompt with your service area, pet-care style, booking process, and meet-and-greet availability.

02

Create your care update format

Build a short message structure: photo note, mood, route/activity, food/water/potty update, and next visit reminder.

03

Set your review ask trigger

Ask after the third successful walk, the end of a pet sitting stay, or a happy first month of recurring service.

04

Add one referral partner touch

Reach out to one groomer, trainer, vet office, or pet shop with a friendly intro and clear referral fit.
