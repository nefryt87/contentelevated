Standard Service Call

Dispatcher Script — Standard Inbound

DISPATCHER: [Company Name], this is [Name] — what's going on for you today? [Listen fully. Don't interrupt. Let them describe the problem completely.] DISPATCHER: Got it — that's something we can take care of. Let me get a few details so I can send the right person. [Collect: name, address, callback number, problem, urgency — running water? wet area?] DISPATCHER: We have [time available]. Our plumbers are licensed and insured — they'll call 30 minutes before arrival. Does that work? [Confirm everything. Read back the address.] Emergency Burst Pipe / Active Leak

04

Working page

Dispatcher Script — Emergency Leak

DISPATCHER: [Company Name] — sounds like an emergency. Where are you located? [Get address immediately. Water damage is time-sensitive. Every minute matters.] DISPATCHER: First thing — if you can safely reach your main water shutoff, turn it off now. It's usually near the water meter, under a sink, or in the basement. Do you know where yours is? [Guide them through shutoff while dispatching the nearest tech simultaneously] DISPATCHER: I'm reaching out to our nearest tech right now. We'll have someone heading your way within [timeframe]. [Call back within 5 minutes with confirmed ETA. Helping the customer stop the water before the tech arrives is a genuine differentiator.] Price-Shopping Call

Dispatcher Script — How Much Do You Charge?

CUSTOMER: How much do you charge to [fix/replace/repair]? DISPATCHER: Great question — I want to give you a real number, not a guess. For water heater replacement, most customers pay between $[X] and $[X] depending on the unit. For repairs, it depends on what we find on-site.
