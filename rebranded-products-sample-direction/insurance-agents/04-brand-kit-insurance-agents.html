Section 1: Brand Positioning

Your brand is not your logo. It's how people feel when they interact with you. For insurance agents, trust is the product. Everything visual should reinforce that you are reliable, clear, and human. Your Brand Promise (Fill in the Blank) I help [target client] protect [what matters most to them] so they can [emotional benefit — e.g., 'sleep at night knowing their family is covered']. Example: 'I help young families protect their home and income so they can focus on building their life — not worrying about what could go wrong.'

Section 5: Logo & Headshot Guidelines

Headshot Best Practices For insurance agents, your headshot IS your brand. It's the first thing people see on Google, LinkedIn, and Facebook. Follow these guidelines: • Background: Solid color or clean office setting — no busy backgrounds • Outfit: Business casual or business professional — avoid busy patterns • Expression: Genuine smile — approachable, not stiff • Lighting: Natural light from a window or a simple ring light — avoid harsh overhead lighting • Framing: Head and shoulders, slightly off-center — professional but not passport-photo stiff • Consistency: Use the SAME headshot across all platforms for brand recognition Logo Usage • Always use your agency or carrier logo at the same size relative to your headshot • On social: Your headshot > logo — people buy from people • On print: Logo gets equal or more prominence — print is about the agency brand • Minimum logo size: Never display smaller than 1 inch wide in print; 100px on digital • File formats: Keep a
