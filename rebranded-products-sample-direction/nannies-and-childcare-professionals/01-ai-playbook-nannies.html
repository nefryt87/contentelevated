Introduction

The nannies and childcare professionals securing premium placements and long-term family relationships aren't just the most experienced with children. They present themselves professionally from the very first inquiry — with clear rates, a compelling bio, and the communication skills that make families feel immediately at ease. They navigate interviews and trials with confidence. They set clear expectations through professional agreements that protect both parties. They build a presence on care platforms and LinkedIn that attracts inbound inquiries from exactly the right families. And they create a working relationship so warm and professional that every family becomes a source of references and referrals. This playbook covers every high- leverage scenario for employed nannies, nanny-share arrangements, and self- employed childcare professionals.

Prompt — Rate Discussion Email

Write a professional rate discussion email for my nanny services. My rate: $ [amount] per hour / gross per week. I want to: (1) present my rate clearly without apologising for it, (2) briefly explain what experience and qualifications inform this rate, (3) describe what the family gets — my hours, my approach, any included activities or educational planning, (4) ask about their budget range to see if we're aligned. Professional and warm. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name].

03

Working page

Prompt — Rate Negotiation Response

Write a professional response to a family who said my nanny rate is higher than their budget. My rate: $[amount]. Their budget: $[amount]. I want to: (1) acknowledge their position without compromising my rate unnecessarily, (2) briefly explain what my rate reflects, (3) explore whether there's a scope adjustment that could work — e.g., fewer hours, different schedule, nanny share arrangement. Warm and professional. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name]. The Working Agreement A clear written working agreement protects both the nanny and the family. Nannies who propose a written agreement signal professionalism and prevent the most common sources of conflict — expectations around duties, sick leave, holidays, and notice periods.

Prompt — Working Agreement Introduction Email

Write an email to a family introducing the idea of a nanny working agreement before we begin. I want to: (1) frame the agreement as beneficial for both of us — clarity protects everyone, (2) mention the key areas it covers — hours, rate, duties, holiday entitlement, sick leave, notice period, trial period, (3) invite them to review a draft and suggest any additions. Professional and non-threatening. Under 150 words. Sign as [Your Name].
