Retention Starts Before The Appointment Ends. How You Run Your Chair Determines Whether A Client

books again — or quietly tries someone else. This system gives you a consistent framework for every appointment. The 5-Part Appointment Framework 1 The Arrival Greeting (first 2 minutes) • Use their name immediately — 'Hi [name], so great to see you!' • Reference something personal from last visit or their intake form: 'How did that balayage hold up in the summer?' • Offer water, coffee, or tea before they sit down — this is hospitality, not just service • Never start talking business (rebooking, retail) in the first 2 minutes 2 The Consultation (even for regulars) • Every appointment gets a brief consultation — even for clients you've seen 50 times • Ask: 'How has your hair been behaving since last time?' — this uncovers maintenance issues and opens retail conversations • Ask: 'Are we doing the same thing today, or is there anything you want to try differently?' • Listen more than you talk in this phase — what they tell you shapes the whole appointment

The Loyalty & Referral System Your most loyal clients are your most valuable marketing asset. They refer friends, leave reviews, and stay through price increases because they trust you. This system is designed to recognize, reward, and activate them. Identifying Your VIP Clients A VIP client meets at least 2 of these criteria: • Books 4+ times per year • Average ticket is above your menu average • Has referred at least one friend in the past year • Has left a Google review • Has been coming to you for 2+ years The 3 Referral Program Structures Structure How It Works Best For Give $20, Get $20 Referring client gets $20 off next visit when their friend books. New client gets $20 off first visit. Easy to explain and track. Works for any salon size. Service Upgrade Reward Referring client gets a complimentary add-on (gloss, scalp treatment, blowout) on their next visit. Protects margins better than discounts. Feels more premium.
