Prompt 1: Same-Day Inquiry Response

Write a warm, professional inquiry response email for my interior design studio. The potential client [Name] reached out about [project type — e.g., full home redesign / kitchen renovation / new build furnishing]. They mentioned: [paste 2-3 key details from their inquiry]. My design style is [your style — e.g., warm minimalism / eclectic maximalism / transitional]. I want to: (1) acknowledge their specific project, (2) briefly describe how I'd approach it, (3) ask one qualifying question about their timeline and investment range, (4) invite them to a 20-minute discovery call. Warm and confident. Under 200 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

Prompt 2: Monthly Realtor Value Touch

Write a monthly value email from an interior designer to a realtor referral partner named [Name] at [Brokerage]. Share: (1) one interior design or staging insight relevant to the current market, (2) one trend their buyers or sellers should know about. Professional, genuinely useful, under 150 words. It should feel like useful information from a knowledgeable colleague — not a marketing email. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].

Prompt 3: Post-Discovery Call Follow-Up

Write a post-discovery call follow-up email for my interior design studio. I just spoke with [Name] about their [project type]. Key things they shared: [list 3-4 things — goals, concerns, lifestyle, timeline]. I'm going to propose [scope description] at [investment range or flat fee]. Before the proposal lands, I want this email to: (1) mirror back their stated goals, (2) briefly explain my process and why it produces better outcomes than DIY or a cheaper option, (3) set the expectation that my proposal will arrive in [timeframe]. Warm and confident. Under 250 words. Sign as [Your Name], [Studio Name].
